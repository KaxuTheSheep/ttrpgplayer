A Rift is a spatial tear between two connected worlds — a point where the boundary between dimensional spaces has thinned enough that passage between them becomes possible. Rifts are not conjured from nothing. They form at natural weak points where the spatial barrier between worlds is already compromised, and they can be forced open, stabilised, or in theory closed by mages with sufficient understanding of spatial mana manipulation.

## How Rifts Form
Space has physical properties subject to mana influence. Where two worlds exist in close dimensional proximity, the barrier between them can thin naturally over time, particularly where ambient mana density is high enough to destabilise local spatial properties. These weak points are not evenly distributed — they form where dimensional geometry happens to place two worlds near one another, which is a matter of cosmological circumstance rather than anything predictable or engineered.

When a weak point thins sufficiently, it tears. The resulting rift connects the two worlds at that point, allowing matter, energy, and mana to pass between them. Once open, a natural rift tends to remain open — the spatial tear is self-sustaining as long as the conditions that produced it persist.

## Time and Connected Worlds
Worlds connected by a rift do not necessarily share the same time rate. Each world has its own relationship with time, and when two worlds are connected, their time rates exert equilibrium pressure on one another — each pulling the other toward its own rate. The degree of influence depends on the relative time rates involved and the strength of the connection.

Mana naturally mediates this pressure. Dense ambient mana slows the propagation of time equilibrium effects, acting as a passive buffer between connected worlds. Without this buffer, time rate differences would propagate at the speed of light. With it, the effect travels far slower — in the case of Solasfall's world, roughly the speed of a running person.

## The Two Known Rifts

### The Fallows Rift
A natural weak point on the opposite side of Solasfall's planet from the city itself. At some point in history it tore open, connecting Solasfall's world to a world where time moves at catastrophic speed — approximately one second outside the Veil equating to a century of experienced time. The connected world's time rate began pulling Solasfall's world toward synchronisation the moment the rift opened.

The effect propagated outward from the rift's location slowly, mediated by natural mana density, spreading at roughly human running pace. Over decades it converted the surrounding land into the Fallows — terrain experiencing such accelerated time that organic matter decays before it can process its own existence. By the time approximately half the planet had been consumed, word reached Solasfall via horseback and boat. The city began accepting refugees and engineering a solution.

The Fallows rift remains open. It is a permanent natural tear that nobody can reach to close. The distance that gave Solasfall time to survive is the same distance that makes intervention impossible. Mages with current knowledge of spatial manipulation could theoretically close it — but closing a rift requires proximity to it, and the Fallows between Solasfall and the rift is impassable.

### The Hollowdeep Rift
A natural weak point beneath or near Solasfall connecting to the Shattered Expanse — the outermost realm of the Hollowdeep's webbed network. Unlike the Fallows rift, this one was discovered intact and exploitable rather than catastrophic in its effects. The Hollowdeep's collective time rate across its many interconnected realms averages to approximately seven times Solasfall's — fast enough to be useful for extended expeditions, slow enough to be survivable.

The Riftconjurors do not create this connection. They force it open temporarily by exploiting the existing weak point, channelling coordinated mana input to overcome the spatial barrier's natural resistance. This requires partially weakening the Veil — the two operations compete for the same mana environment. The rift is held open for one hour every four weeks, during which time flows equally between Solasfall and the Hollowdeep. Once closed, the Hollowdeep's natural time rate resumes and the 7:1 ratio applies until the rift is forced open again.

## The Hollowdeep Dependency
Mordant Stones harvested from the Hollowdeep are the primary supply sustaining the Veil's Mordant mana field. Without a continuous supply, the field weakens, the time equilibrium pressure from the Fallows rift increases, and eventually the Veil fails. If the Veil fails, the Fallows consumes Solasfall at the same accelerated rate as the rest of the world.

The controlled Hollowdeep rift is therefore not an economic convenience — it is an existential necessity. Solasfall survives because it can harvest Mordant Stones. It can harvest Mordant Stones because the Riftconjurors can open the connection. If that capability were lost, the city's supply would deplete and the Veil would eventually collapse.

## Controlled vs Natural Rifts
A natural rift is self-sustaining once open. A controlled rift — like the Hollowdeep connection — is forced open against natural resistance and closes when the mana input is withdrawn. Maintaining a controlled rift indefinitely would require continuous coordinated mana input beyond current institutional capacity. The four-week cycle is a practical limit, not a chosen one.

Small-scale spatial rifts — brief, localised tears connecting two nearby points — are achievable by individuals with sufficient understanding and control, though difficult to stabilise. These do not require a pre-existing weak point but are limited in range and duration by the mana required to overcome local spatial resistance without the advantage of a natural thin point.
