Mana Stones are minerals crystallised into a stable excitation state by prolonged exposure to concentrated mana currents. They are not vessels that contain mana — they are materials whose physical structure has been permanently shaped by extreme mana conditions, holding a fixed excitation charge that interacts with mana flowing through or near them.

## Formation
Under normal ambient conditions, mana currents are too diffuse to affect physical matter in lasting ways. In areas where currents concentrate — whether through geological formations, rift proximity, or unusual mana density — the sustained pressure of heavily excited mana gradually alters the molecular structure of surrounding minerals. Over time, the material crystallises around the excitation state it was exposed to, becoming a stable expression of that charge.

In Solasfall's world, the conditions for this are rare. Naturally occurring Mana Stones exist but are uncommon, forming only where mana currents happen to concentrate consistently over long periods.

In the **Hollowdeep**, where the World's Breath flows through an interconnected web of permanently open rifts, mana density is dramatically higher. The resulting conditions produce Mana Stones in abundance, and at excitation intensities rarely matched by surface formations. This abundance is the primary economic reason Hollowdeep expeditions are sanctioned and funded by the city.

## Types

### Vivant Stones
Crystallised under Vivant excitation conditions. When mana flows through or near a Vivant Stone, it is pushed toward Vivant excitation — accelerating natural processes in the surrounding area. Used to drive combustion, accelerate healing, fuel growth, and power mechanisms requiring energetic output.

### Mordant Stones
Crystallised under Mordant excitation conditions. Mordant Stones push nearby mana toward Mordant excitation — decelerating natural processes. Used in containment, preservation, suppression effects, and large-scale structural magic such as the Veil's barrier function.

### Inert Stones
A depleted Mana Stone that has returned to equilibrium. The crystalline structure remains, but its excitation influence has faded through sustained use. Inert Stones are not destroyed — under the right conditions of prolonged mana exposure, they can re-crystallise into either a Vivant or Mordant state depending on the currents present. Whether this is economically viable is a matter of ongoing institutional research.

## Excitation Intensity
Not all Mana Stones are equal. The intensity of a stone's excitation state — how far from inert it sits — depends on the concentration and duration of mana currents it formed under.

A weakly excited Vivant Stone produces mild acceleration effects and depletes quickly under heavy use. A highly excited stone produces dramatic effects and sustains them far longer. This variance is the primary driver of Mana Stone valuation, and the reason standardised currency proved more practical than using stones directly in trade.

Hollowdeep stones tend toward higher intensity than surface formations, though this varies significantly by location within the web. Different areas produce stones of different excitation types and intensities depending on local mana conditions, making expedition intelligence — knowing which realms yield which stones — genuinely valuable information.

## Handling & Risks
A Mana Stone does not inherently endanger its handler. The excitation influence is gradual and ambient under normal conditions.

The risk arises when a stone is damaged, rapidly depleted, or exposed to mana conditions that conflict violently with its crystallised state. In these cases the stone's structure can fail unpredictably, releasing its stored excitation in an uncontrolled burst. This is a common cause of **Mana Overload Syndrome** among inexperienced handlers, and a known occupational hazard in both the Explorers' Guild and the Magic Institutions.

Forcing a stone against its excitation state — attempting to use a Mordant Stone as a Vivant source, or vice versa — accelerates structural degradation and significantly increases the risk of catastrophic failure.

## Economic Role
Mana Stones are the economic foundation of Solasfall's magical infrastructure. The Veil's maintenance, the Rift cycle, institutional research, and most large-scale magical operations depend on a steady supply.

They once served directly as currency, measured and exchanged via Mana Scales. This proved impractical — excitation intensity is difficult to verify in casual exchange, and the variance between stones of nominally the same type made consistent valuation unreliable. The Ministry of Balance eventually standardised conversion: stones are brought to verified assay points, assessed for type and intensity, and exchanged for **Gilded Mints** at pegged rates. The Ministry controls these rates entirely, giving them significant leverage over both the Explorers' Guild and the Magic Institutions.
