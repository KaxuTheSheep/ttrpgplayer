**Purpose**  
The Weighing is the central economic and social control mechanism of Solasfall — a relentless, city-wide tax collection enforced with total, fatal precision. Pegged directly to the Mana Stones brought back from the Hollowdeep, it ensures that citizens are either economically productive or expendable.

### Mechanics
**Tax Amounts**  
Each citizen, regardless of age, race, or occupation, is assigned a tax quota in Gilded Mints. This amount is determined by factors such as:
- District of residence
- Prior delving participation
- Past compliance with The Weighing

**No Exceptions**
- Children pay via their families.
- The elderly, priests, and nobles must also pay.

**Late is Dead**  
Payments must be made on the exact day of the Weighing. Even minor delays are treated as noncompliance. The Ministry employs magical records, scrying networks, and the Veil-based detection systems to track all citizens.

**Enforcement**  
The Ministry relies on the Order of the Gilded Chain — a specialized branch of The Knights Orders — to carry out The Weighing. Responsibilities include:
- Collecting unpaid taxes directly.
- Overseeing arrests and executions according to Ministry protocol.
- Maintaining order during public Weighings.
- Serving as a visible reminder of the city’s unyielding adherence to “balance,” their gilded armor and scales symbolizing authority.

**The Executions**
- One week before the next Rift, the Ministry holds a public Execution Day.
- Citizens who fail to pay are either:
    - Executed immediately in their district, or
    - Paraded to a central plaza for mass execution — a warning to others.
- Anyone resisting arrest is executed on the spot, without trial.
### Social Impact
The Weighing creates a cycle of fear and desperation:
- Many citizens are forced into The Explorers' Guild to harvest Mana Stones to meet quotas.
- Parents send children into the Hollowdeep at the earliest age they can hold a blade or cast a spell.
- Religious groups use The Weighing to preach obedience or incite revolt.
- Nobles often sponsor proxy Explorers or manipulate quotas through bribery.

Some citizens romanticize The Weighing as a “purifying balance,” but most see it as ritualized cruelty disguised as order.

### Official Story
According to the Ministry of Balance:
- The Veil consumes immense mana to remain active and protect Solasfall.
- Mana Stones are the city’s lifeblood; citizens who fail to contribute are leeches.
- “Balance is Survival” — and survival requires sacrifice.
_"To take from the Veil without giving in return is theft. To hoard what feeds us is treason. The coin is not punishment — it is the price of peace."_

### Public Belief
- Few fully believe the official justification.
- Excessive taxation, merciless enforcement, and executions are seen as mechanisms of control, not necessity.
- Nobles benefit from loopholes or protection through bribes, while commoners call the Ministry’s doctrine “Veilwash” and Gilded Mints “Blood Coins.”
- Open rebellion is rare, as dissent carries lethal consequences; exile into the Fallows is considered worse.

**Undercurrents of Dissent**
- The Rebel Group spreads counterpropaganda about mana hoarding, corruption, and the lie of balance.
- Maw Below Cultists mock the system entirely, preaching consumption itself as divine.
