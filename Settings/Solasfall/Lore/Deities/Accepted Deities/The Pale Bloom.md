**Titles:**  
The Enduring Petal, Lady of Suffering, Silent Grace

**Domains:**  
Suffering, Endurance, Tragic Beauty

**Symbols & Iconography:**  
The Pale Bloom is depicted as a fragile, luminous flower growing through stone, or as a delicate figure cloaked in pale, flowing robes with petals trailing like whispers. Her symbol is a single white bloom, often carved into walls or worn as a pendant.

**Mythos:**  
The Pale Bloom embodies survival through hardship, teaching that beauty and meaning can arise from suffering. Legends say she walks unseen among the poor and the downtrodden, carrying the weight of their burdens and whispering courage to the resilient. Her stories stress patience, quiet strength, and the grace of endurance, showing that suffering is not meaningless but a crucible that shapes the soul.

**Influence & Presence:**  
Temples and shrines are humble yet serene, often hidden in slums, workshops, or gardens, spaces where the overlooked can seek solace. Her guidance is subtle, granting insight and resilience to those who endure without complaint. Followers invoke her presence in quiet moments, in art, or in acts of perseverance rather than grand ceremonies.

**Worshippers:**  
Revered by the poor, artists, and those who endure hardship silently. Her faithful often find strength in community and personal discipline. See Pale Bloom Sanctum.
