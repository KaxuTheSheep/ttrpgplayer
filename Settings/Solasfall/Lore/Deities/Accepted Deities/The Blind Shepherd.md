**Titles:**  
The Veiled Guide, Keeper of Oaths, Warden of the Dead

**Domains:**  
Death, Law, Guidance, Vows, Exchange

**Symbols & Iconography:**  
Depicted as a hooded figure with their eyes bound by linen and sheep horns protrude from his hood. In his hands lay a cane and scales. Candles are often placed before their likeness, representing souls finding light in the dark.

**Mythos:**  
The Blind Shepherd is said to walk the boundary between the living and the dead, guiding souls across the Veil to their rightful rest. In Death, all must answer to their judgement, where every vow sworn in life is weighed against the truth of one’s deeds. It is believed the Shepherd cannot see falsehoods — only the weight of Actions themselves.

**Influence & Presence:**  
Their influence reaches every layer of Solasfall, from the quiet prayers of mourners to the oaths sworn in courtrooms. Shrines to the Shepherd are often found at crossroads, prisons, and graveyards, where the boundary between worlds feels thin.

**Worshippers:**  
Judges, executioners, oathbound knights, wayfinders, and merchants seek the Shepherd’s favour in their dealings with truth, Death, and promise. See Blind Shepherd Sanctum.
