**Titles:**  
The Green Father, The Blooming Fortune, Keeper of Cycles

**Domains:**  
Harvest, Growth, Luck

**Symbols & Iconography:**  
The Verdant Hand is often represented by an open palm sprouting green shoots or wrapped in vines. Shrines in his honour are decorated with woven grass charms, small bundles of grain, and carved wooden hands placed in soil as offerings.

**Mythos:**  
It is said that when the world first learned to starve, the Verdant Hand taught mortals to nurture rather than take. From his fingertips, crops sprouted in the ash of fallen empires. Yet his favour is fickle — as the seasons turn, so too does fortune. Those who boast of his blessing often find their harvests turned to rot.

**Influence & Presence:**  
The Verdant Hand’s faith is one of reciprocity: what is taken must be returned. Farmers and merchants offer tithes to his name before each harvest or deal, while gamblers whisper his prayers before a throw of dice. Though considered a kindly god, his wrath manifests in blight, drought, and misfortune for those who hoard rather than share.

**Worshippers:**  
He is followed by farmers, labourers, and the superstitious — anyone who understands that prosperity and ruin often grow from the same soil. See Verdant Hand Sanctum.
