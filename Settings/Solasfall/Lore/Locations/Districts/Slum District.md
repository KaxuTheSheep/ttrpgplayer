The Slum District is a tangled warren of collapsing buildings, makeshift housing, and flickering lanterns. It is home to the city’s outcasts, poorest citizens, and marginalised peoples — primarily **Cindral**, **Stitched**, and **Velkhari**, though a few desperate humans and other Races live here too. The district is a place of both quiet suffering and defiant resilience.

### Key Features
**Makeshift Housing & Crumbling Architecture**
- The district is a patchwork of abandoned buildings, scavenged materials, and half-sunk stonework from older city expansions.
- Many homes are built vertically, using scrap and rope bridges, creating a vertical shanty town over older ruins.
- Lack of proper plumbing or infrastructure leads to constant issues with disease, flooding, and waste.

**The Stitched Wards**
- The Stitched — reanimated or created beings — dwell in a quiet, walled section of the Slum, often segregated by fear and misunderstanding.
- This ward has an eerie stillness, punctuated by quiet maintenance rituals and whispered prayers to Deities no longer sanctioned.

**The Cindral Enclaves**
- Cindral dominate the political and cultural heart of the slums, forming tight-knit families, black markets, and unofficial leaders.
- Some enclaves are deeply spiritual, practicing forms of forbidden or ancestral magic kept alive in secrecy.

**The Velkhari Shadowspaces**
- Velkhari, with their strange physiology and traditions, have carved out shadowy grottos and hidden gathering spaces where they speak in smoke and symbol.
- Most citizens avoid these areas entirely, calling them “whisper pits.”

### Culture & Survival

**Informal Power Structures**
- With no official governance, the slums run on gang hierarchies, cult cells, and community leaders.
- Some work with the Thieves' Guild or Rebel Group, while others just try to protect their Block.

**Black Markets**
- Smuggled Hollowdeep artefacts, stolen mana stones, banned texts, and illegal alchemy — if it cannot be sold elsewhere, it ends up here.

**Street Faith & Cultic Blends**
- Strange, syncretic beliefs thrive — some mix sanctioned faiths with forbidden gods, while others follow lost spirits or local saints.

**Knight Patrols Rare**
- Knights only enter in large groups or during public executions.
- Most law enforcement is handled by gangs, mercs, or priests.
### Public Perception
- Viewed as dangerous and dirty by the upper classes.
- Many nobles advocate for “cleansing the slum” or “reforming it,” though few offer real help.
- Yet many innovations, underground artists, and even some heroes emerge from the Slum, forged by hardship and fury.
