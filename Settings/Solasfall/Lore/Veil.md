The Veil is an immense magical structure encasing Solasfall, separating it from the accelerated decay of the world beyond its boundaries. To most citizens it appears as a natural sky — sun, clouds, stars, and weather cycling in familiar rhythms. In truth it is two distinct systems operating in concert: a barrier that keeps time inside Solasfall from racing toward ruin, and a controlled sky layer that the Royal Mages actively operate to simulate and manipulate conditions above the city.

## The Time Problem
The world outside Solasfall is not simply dangerous — it is dying at speed. Through the same rift phenomenon that connects worlds, Solasfall's world is in equilibrium contact with a realm where time moves at a dramatically accelerated rate. Everything outside the Veil experiences this — decay, growth, and entropy rushing forward simultaneously. Those who step beyond the boundary do not perceive their own acceleration. Their minds experience time normally while their bodies age and deteriorate in moments.

The Veil's primary function is to suppress this effect inside the city. It achieves this through an enormous sustained field of Mordant-excited mana, decreasing the equilibrium pull from the fast-time connection and maintaining something close to normal temporal conditions within Solasfall's boundaries. Without it, the city would gradually synchronise with the world outside.

## Construction
The Veil was conceived and executed during the reign of the current King, in collaboration with the Royal Mages. It stands as the greatest magical undertaking in recorded history.

Its foundation is a vast interlayered network of magic circles — a primary circle encompassing the entire city with hundreds of smaller interlocking circles nested within it. Each smaller circle draws in ambient mana from the surrounding environment and channels it into the overall structure. The network does not require mages to power it directly. It is infrastructure, not instrument — designed to run continuously with operators monitoring and adjusting flow rather than sustaining it through effort.

Mordant Stones harvested from the Hollowdeep provide the baseline excited mana the network requires. Mages stationed at the circle's anchor points regulate how much mana moves through each section, correcting imbalances and responding to fluctuations as they arise.

The King participated directly in the Veil's final sealing ritual. The scale of mana conducted through a single soul during that moment caused catastrophic **Mana Overload Syndrome**, permanently severing his connection to mana. The Veil held. The cost was his.

## The Barrier
The boundary itself arises from the differential between the Veil's interior and the world outside. Inside, mana is partially isolated from the World's Breath, slowly neutralising toward inert without continuous replenishment from Mordant Stones. Outside, the World's Breath pushes mana to extremes unimpeded. The sharp boundary between these two states creates natural resistance — not a wall, but a pressure gradient that becomes increasingly difficult to cross the closer one approaches the Veil's edge.

From within the city this is invisible. At the fringes the sky layer thins and the mana density becomes perceptible even to the untrained — a heaviness in the air, a wrongness in the light. Those who press further find the boundary mist-like and total, and beyond it, brief glimpses of the Fallows: the bleeding hues of a world that has already run too far forward.

## The Sky Layer
Separate from the barrier, the Royal Mages maintain a controlled Vivant mana field that physically manipulates light across the interior of the Veil. This is not illusion — it is literal manipulation of light's properties, adjusting wavelengths, amplitudes, and polarisation to produce the appearance of sun, cloud, stars, and weather.

The sky layer requires active management rather than passive maintenance. Royal Mages operate it continuously in rotation, which gives them — and through them, the Royal Advisors — direct control over the apparent conditions inside Solasfall at any given moment.

This control is exercised routinely for political and social purposes. The day-night cycle is standard. Weather patterns are adjusted for agriculture and public events. Sunrise and sunset can be delayed or advanced. A noble family hosting an event under a specific sky can petition — and pay — for those conditions to be arranged. The Royal Advisors have been known to associate particular light conditions with decrees or ceremonies, creating an implicit language of divine favour written in weather.

The sky layer also extends beyond the city's outer walls, sustaining farms and rivers that would otherwise be exposed to the unfiltered time acceleration of the outside world.

## Staffing & Operations
The Veil's circle network operates with approximately twenty to thirty mages on shift at any given time, drawn from a rotation pool of around eighty to one hundred combining Royal Mages and senior institutional mages. Routine monitoring shifts are handled by senior institutional mages — the work requires precision and familiarity with the circle network rather than exceptional magical power. Royal Mages take primary responsibility during the four-week renewal ceremonies, emergency corrections, and sky layer operations, which demand a higher level of active control.

The rotation is carefully managed. Losing even a small number of qualified operators to Hollowdeep expeditions or injury creates measurable strain on shift coverage, which is one of the less publicised pressures the Ministry of Balance manages through its resource allocation.

## Instability & The Rift Cycle
Every four weeks, the Royal Mages deliberately weaken the Veil to force open the controlled rift to the Hollowdeep. The two operations compete for the same mana environment — the Riftconjurors require coordinated mana input to overcome the spatial barrier's natural resistance at the weak point, while the Veil's Mordant field requires its own continuous supply to function. Partially withdrawing the Veil's mana to accommodate the rift opening is an accepted and managed cost, not an accident.

During this window the barrier's Mordant field is partially disrupted. Citizens experience this as distortions in the sky — flickering light, brief crimson flashes, distorted shapes where the sky layer fails to compensate. Some report sounds with no source. The official designation is _mana storms_, attributed to natural fluctuation. The Royal Mages know better.

Full renewal of the Veil's structure occurs on the same four-week schedule, aligned with the Rift cycle. These ceremonies are citywide events drawing both reverence and unease. They are the moments when the Veil's fragility is most visible and rumours about what would happen if it failed circulate most freely.

## Maintenance & Cost
The Veil's continuous operation demands enormous quantities of Mordant Stones. The Ministry of Balance manages procurement and funding through taxation and resource tithes levied across Solasfall. This economic burden falls heaviest on the poorer districts, who pay the most proportionally and benefit least from the sky layer's political uses.

Dissent is growing. The Rebel Group is divided — some believe freedom lies beyond the Veil, not understanding or disbelieving the time acceleration that waits outside. Others know exactly what the Veil prevents and fear that weakening it for any reason courts catastrophe.

## Rumours & Misconceptions
Among the lower districts, persistent rumours claim the Veil is sustained not by Mana Stones and rotating mages but by souls — hundreds of mages permanently bound into its structure, their consciousness trapped inside the barrier, experiencing every moment of the centuries since its creation.

This is false. The Veil runs on infrastructure and shift operators, not sacrifice. The rumour likely originated from the King's very public Mana Overload during the sealing ritual, which people extrapolated into something far darker. The Royal Mages have never formally denied it. A population that believes the Veil demands that kind of cost is a population less likely to question its maintenance fees.

## Public Perception
To most citizens the Veil is a miracle — the great shield that allows civilisation to exist. Children are raised under its painted stars. Poets praise its ever-living dawn. The renewal ceremonies are occasions of genuine reverence.

The sky layer reinforces this. A population that watches the sun rise and set on a familiar rhythm, that sees rain come when crops need it, that lives under a sky that feels *managed* rather than arbitrary — that population does not question the Veil's necessity. This is not accidental.
