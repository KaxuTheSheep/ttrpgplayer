# Status
*Scorned, divided, and spiritually conflicted.*
Once followers of the Emberwright, Cindral are seen by many as betrayers of that deity and now primarily revere the Pale Bloom. They occupy a precarious social standing, scattered throughout the city but mostly concentrated in their own district. Known for their passion, resilience, and deep religious fervour, they hold tightly to their cultural identity despite widespread suspicion. Cindral fill diverse roles as artisans, merchants, warriors, and religious devotees.
# Culture
Cindral culture centres on endurance through suffering and redemption through faith in the Pale Bloom. Their shorter lifespans encourage them to live fiercely and with purpose, embracing both hardship and community. Traditions emphasise loyalty to the Pale Bloom and personal resilience over old Emberwright worship. Family ties vary but often revolve around shared belief and protecting one another from outside scorn.

They cultivate physical skill and craftsmanship as expressions of their enduring spirit. Their district is a centre for artisanship, religious gatherings, and underground resistance to outside oppression.
## Relationships with Other Races
Humans are viewed with a mix of resentment and grudging respect, especially for their social dominance. Kenku are tolerated but seen as distant and less spiritually committed. Murks earn some respect for their quiet endurance and connection to nature. Velkhari are considered reckless and unreliable, though respected for their combat prowess. Stitched inspire both pity and curiosity — reminders of survival at great cost. Drenek are regarded as strong but lacking the spiritual depth Cindral prize.
# Appearance
Cindral possess obsidian-like skin that shifts in opacity, from glossy black glass to smoke-clouded grey. Beneath the surface, faint cracks glow with inner fire, pulsing in rhythm with their breath or emotion. Their eyes burn with steady flame, varying in hue from deep ember to pale white-gold, betraying mood and vitality. Hair, when present, appears as ashen strands or faintly glowing filaments, often braided or bound to avoid catching alight during intense work.

Though humanoid in shape, their movements are deliberate and almost engineered — a reflection of their constructed grace and inner discipline. Many wear heat-resistant fabrics, tempered metal, or volcanic glass adornments, combining function and reverence for their fiery origin. In darkness, a Cindral's body emits a subtle ember-like glow along their fractures — a haunting reminder of both their fragility and endurance.

# Traits
## Emberlight
Your skin glows with a faint fiery crackle, shedding dim light in a 3m radius around you. This cannot be suppressed voluntarily — it is a biological constant, not a magical effect.

## Heat Resistance
Your fire-touched physiology reduces all incoming fire damage by 2. This reflects biological tolerance rather than magical protection.

## Emberstrike
When you make a melee or ranged attack, you may choose to channel your body heat through your weapon as part of that attack, adding +1 fire damage. This is a biological output, not a magical effect, and has no usage limit.

## Fireforged Endurance
Gain +1 training in one of the following skills of your choice at character creation: Athletics, Crafting, or Endurance.

# Racial Web
## Channelled Emberstrike
Prerequisites: None.
Cost: 2 Trait Points. 
You learn to concentrate your body heat into a single focused burst. As a Full Action, you may channel heat through your weapon, dealing weapon damage + 1d6 fire damage. Once per Short Rest.

## Dimmed Emberlight 
Prerequisites: None. 
Cost: 1 Trait Point. 
You learn to partially suppress your internal glow. Your Emberlight radius reduces from 3m to 1.5m at will. You may toggle this freely.

## Tempered Resistance
Prerequisites: None.
Cost: 2 Trait Points.
Your heat tolerance increases. Fire damage reduction increases from 2 to 4.

## Swift Channel
Prerequisites: Channelled Emberstrike. 
Cost: 2 Trait Points. 
Your channelling technique becomes faster. Channelled Emberstrike now costs a Half Action instead of a Full Action.

## Sustained Burn
Prerequisites: Channelled Emberstrike.
Cost: 2 Trait Points. 
Your body sustains the heat output longer. Channelled Emberstrike charges increase to Vitality per Short Rest, minimum 1.

## Reinforced Resistance
Prerequisites: Tempered Resistance. 
Cost: 2 Trait Points.
Your heat tolerance reaches its biological ceiling. Fire damage reduction increases from 4 to 6.

## Nurturing Flame
Prerequisites: Reinforced Resistance.
Cost: 2 Trait Points. 
Your body now converts absorbed fire energy into vitality. Whenever incoming fire damage is reduced to zero or below by your Heat Resistance, the excess converts to Blood restoration rather than being lost.
> For example, taking 4 fire damage with 6 reduction heals 2 Blood.

## Emberwhip
Prerequisites: Sustained Burn, Exclusive to Blastic Arrow. 
Cost: 3 Trait Points.
You learn to release your channelled heat as a forward arc of flame on melee attacks. When you use Channelled Emberstrike with a melee weapon, the strike releases a whip-like arc of fire that extends the attack's reach by 5m for that strike. The arc travels in a straight line — any creature or solid object between you and the target intercepts it first. Uses the same charge pool as Channelled Emberstrike. If you carry ranged weapons, standard Emberstrike applies to them as normal.

## Blastic Arrow
Prerequisites: Sustained Burn, Exclusive to Emberwhip.
Cost: 3 Trait Points. 
You learn to supercharge ranged projectiles with concentrated heat, causing them to detonate on impact. When you use Channelled Emberstrike with a ranged weapon, the projectile explodes on collision with any logical surface — including terrain, water excluded. The explosion deals 1d8 fire damage in a 2m radius. Creatures hit by both the projectile and the explosion take both sources of damage. Uses the same charge pool as Channelled Emberstrike. If you carry melee weapons, standard Emberstrike applies to them as normal.

## Suppressed Emberlight 
Prerequisites: Dimmed Emberlight. 
Cost: 2 Trait Points. 
You gain full control over your internal glow. You may suppress your Emberlight entirely at will, shedding no light whatsoever. You may toggle freely between all states.

## Crafting Affinity

Prerequisites: None.
Cost: 2 Trait Points. 
Your fire-touched nature lends itself naturally to the forge and the workshop. You have Advantage on all Crafting Tests. Time spent crafting is halved. Items you craft gain +3 to any Test or check that would destroy or damage them.
