# Currency & Alternatives

*What people actually use when they stop trusting [[The Reserve]].*

---

## The Problem

[[The Reserve]] is the official currency issuer for New Halcyon and the broader Federal States. It is also visibly losing credibility — selling off its own operations, allowing inflation to creep, and no longer functioning as a genuine monetary authority. For populations in stable, wealthy districts this is a background annoyance managed through diversification. For populations in poorer and forgotten districts it is an active material threat.

---

## What Has Filled the Gap

**Cryptocurrency** — decentralised, Reserve-independent, already mainstream in New Halcyon's more technically literate communities. Multiple competing currencies exist; none is dominant across the whole city. Conglomerates accept the more stable ones for certain transactions; the Reserve technically still insists on primacy for large contracts, but enforcement is nonexistent.

**Closed-community currencies** — localised exchange systems operating within specific districts, faction territories, or building complexes. Backed by local trust and local enforcement rather than any broader institution. More stable within their community than the Reserve in some cases; worthless outside of it. Influenced partly by [[Country E]]'s decentralised economic models filtering through diaspora and cultural exchange.

**Conglomerate scrip** — some conglomerates issue their own internal currency or credit systems for use within their own properties and subsidiaries. Not universally accepted but stable within conglomerate territory. Creates an additional dependency layer — residents of conglomerate-owned housing may find conglomerate scrip more useful than Reserve currency for daily life, which is not accidental.

---

## Class Dimension

Currency use is a class marker in New Halcyon:
- Wealthy and corporate-adjacent: diversified holdings, crypto reserves, Reserve currency for formal transactions
- Middle tier: Reserve currency primary, some crypto, aware of alternatives
- Poor districts: closed-community currencies and barter increasingly dominant; Reserve currency held as little as possible given inflation exposure
