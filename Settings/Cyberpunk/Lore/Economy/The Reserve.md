# The Reserve

*The privatised former central bank. New Halcyon's — and the Federal States' — currency issuer. Slowly selling itself into irrelevance.*

---

## What It Is

The Reserve is what remains of the Federal States' original central bank after [[The Catalyst (2039)]] forced its wholesale privatisation as a bailout condition. It issues and mints the primary currency used across New Halcyon and the broader Federal States. It has no government backing it. It has no reserve in any meaningful sense. It has the name, the infrastructure, and the inertia of institutional trust — all of which are eroding.

---

## Behaviour

The Reserve has historically behaved with more responsibility than its ownership structure would suggest — acting quasi-independently to preserve currency credibility, because a worthless currency is bad for its own business model. It is not altruistic; it is self-interested in a way that happens to align with public stability.

That is changing. The Reserve is now beginning to sell off pieces of its own operation — data divisions, regional minting facilities, policy advisory arms — to service its own debts and satisfy its shareholders. Each sale chips away at the credibility that is its only real asset. The people running it know this. They are doing it anyway.

---

## Consequences

- Inflation is a creeping background condition in New Halcyon
- Crypto adoption and closed-community alternative currencies are growing, especially in poorer districts and ideologically-driven enclaves — see [[Currency & Alternatives]]
- Wealthier actors are quietly diversifying holdings away from Reserve-issued currency
- The Federal States' [[Micronutrient Research]] programme is partly hampered by Reserve instability making long-term public investment harder to finance

---

## Appearance in New Halcyon

The Reserve's branding is everywhere and trusted by nobody — old institutional design language (serious typography, muted colours, language of stability) on currency that everyone knows is slowly deflating. A visual irony that is very on-brand for the city.
