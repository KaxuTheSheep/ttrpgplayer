# Vehicles & Neural Driving

*Self-driving handles navigation. Neural input handles everything else.*

---

## The Two Modes

Vehicles in 2100 New Halcyon operate in two distinct modes that serve entirely different purposes and are not in competition with each other.

**Autonomous navigation** — the vehicle drives itself from point A to point B. Mature technology, unremarkable, the default for routine transit. Passengers interact with destination input rather than driving. The vehicle handles routing, traffic management, obstacle avoidance, and all moment-to-moment operation without human input.

**Neural manual control** — the driver interfaces directly with the vehicle through the seat's wireless neural plate, bypassing conventional steering and pedal inputs entirely. Not a replacement for autonomous navigation. A different mode of vehicle operation altogether, used when the driver needs precision, responsiveness, and control that autonomous systems cannot provide — combat, pursuit, evasion, performance driving in unpredictable conditions.

The distinction is important: neural driving is not a more futuristic way to go to work. It is a combat and control capability that happens to operate a vehicle.

---

## Neural Input — The Seat Plate

The mechanism is an extension of the existing [[Framework|SOMA framework]] infrastructure. The vehicle seat contains a wireless neural plate — a transmitter/receiver array embedded in the seat surface that interfaces with the driver's framework through the same pairing protocol used for other devices.

The driver pairs with the vehicle. The seat plate reads motor intent from the framework's NI/NN output — not raw neural signal but the interpreted intention the framework has already processed. This is forwarded to the vehicle's control systems, which execute the input with the vehicle's own mechanical and electronic response systems handling the physical translation.

**What this produces:** the vehicle responds to where the driver intends to go before the conscious decision to execute a specific steering input has fully formed. The lag between intention and vehicle response approaches zero. At speed, in a pursuit or combat situation, this is a meaningful advantage over any physical control interface.

The vehicle does not become an extension of the nervous system in a literal sense — the driver is not feeling the road through the seat. But the response fidelity is high enough that experienced neural drivers develop an intuitive sense of vehicle state that approaches that.

## Class Texture

Neural driving capability scales with vehicle quality in ways that mirror augment quality.

A well-calibrated vehicle with a current-generation seat plate reads driver intent cleanly and responds precisely. A cheap or poorly maintained vehicle introduces lag or misinterpretation — the vehicle does approximately what the driver intended, with a margin of error that becomes dangerous at speed. The seat plate quality, the vehicle's control system latency, and the calibration of the pairing between driver and vehicle all affect the outcome.

Experienced neural drivers can feel the difference between a quality vehicle and a poor one within seconds of pairing. A driver who usually operates premium hardware in a salvage vehicle is working harder for worse results.

Vehicles in wealthy districts are maintained, current-generation, and respond well. Vehicles in poorer districts are older, poorly maintained, and less predictable. This is not just an economic observation — it affects what is possible in those districts in a vehicle pursuit or combat situation.

## Propulsion

**Electric** — the dominant propulsion method for personal and urban vehicles. Solid state batteries make range a non-issue for daily use. Silent in operation — New Halcyon's ambient sound is tyre noise, air movement, and the hum of high-current systems rather than combustion engine noise. This changes the texture of the city acoustically in ways that become invisible to long-term residents and immediately noticeable to anyone arriving from outside.

**Hydrogen** — viable by 2100 for applications where energy density justifies the complexity. Heavy freight, military vehicles, and high-performance applications where solid state battery weight and volume becomes a constraint. The [[Continental Freight Authority]] almost certainly runs hydrogen logistics infrastructure given the scale of their operation. Hydrogen fuelling infrastructure exists but is less ubiquitous than electric charging.

The energy source question has a political dimension in the Federal States. Hydrogen produced by electrolysis is cheapest when renewable electricity is cheap — which means the UT's West Coast renewable infrastructure makes the UT a natural hydrogen supplier. FS conglomerates that built electric charging infrastructure prefer the status quo because it depends on domestically controlled power generation. A shift to hydrogen dependency would layer an energy dependency on top of the existing food dependency. This has not happened by design rather than accident.

**Military and heavy conglomerate vehicles** may run louder, more powerful systems as a deliberate presence signal. A silent vehicle in New Halcyon is normal. A vehicle that makes noise is either old, cheap, or announcing itself intentionally. Conglomerate Tier 1 force vehicles in certain operational contexts choose the latter.

## Visible Design

Vehicle aesthetics signal class as clearly as anything else in New Halcyon.

Expensive vehicles have no visible mechanical joins — panels flow into each other, grown or printed as unified forms rather than assembled from components. The vehicle reads as a single object rather than a collection of parts.

Cheap and mid-tier vehicles are visibly assembled — panel gaps, mismatched repairs, components from different manufacturers or different eras combined by whoever maintained it last. The history of the vehicle is visible in its body.

Vertical take-off personal vehicles exist at the high end — expensive, restricted to areas with the airspace management infrastructure to handle them, and another visible class marker in a city where how you move signals who you are.
