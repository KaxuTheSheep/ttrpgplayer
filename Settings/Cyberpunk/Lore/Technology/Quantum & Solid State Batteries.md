# Quantum & Solid State Batteries

*Two tiers of power technology. One mainstream, one rare. Both significantly beyond present day.*

---

## The Baseline — Solid State Batteries

Solid state batteries replace the liquid electrolyte of current lithium-ion batteries with a solid material. This is not speculative — solid state batteries are in late-stage development today and the engineering problems are being solved. By 2100 they are mature, cheap, and ubiquitous across all consumer and implant applications.

**What solid state provides over current batteries:**
- Higher energy density — more power stored per unit of volume and weight
- Faster charge rates — solid electrolytes handle higher current without degradation
- Longer cycle life — no liquid to degrade, no dendrite formation that kills current batteries over time
- No thermal runaway — no liquid means no fire risk from puncture or overcharge
- Safe for implantation — no liquid electrolyte means no leakage risk into biological tissue

For implanted frameworks and augments, that last point is as important as the performance gains. A battery technology that can be implanted safely and left in the body for years without maintenance is a prerequisite for the augmentation ecosystem your world runs on.

Solid state is the standard across all framework tiers. A Tier 1 framework has a small, lower-capacity solid state battery. A Tier 5 has a larger, higher-capacity one with faster recharge. The technology is the same; the implementation differs by tier.

---

## The High End — Dicke Quantum Batteries

Quantum batteries exploit quantum mechanical effects — specifically quantum coherence and entanglement — to charge and discharge faster than classical batteries and potentially retain energy with lower losses over time. Dicke quantum batteries use collective quantum effects across many particles simultaneously, theoretically enabling charging speeds and discharge rates that classical physics cannot match.

This is real theoretical physics being actively researched. By 2100 it is plausible that quantum batteries exist in functional form, though they remain expensive and difficult to manufacture compared to mature solid state technology.

**Where quantum batteries appear in this world:**
- Tier 4 and Tier 5 framework configurations at the high end
- Military augment packages issued by conglomerate forces
- Specialist weapon systems — railguns and directed energy weapons that require high instantaneous discharge rates that solid state cannot match at compact sizes
- High-end vehicles requiring sustained high-performance power delivery

Quantum batteries are not a consumer product. They are issued, contracted, or black-market acquired rather than purchased off the shelf.

---

## EMP Vulnerability

Quantum batteries rely on quantum coherence — a fragile state that certain electromagnetic environments can disrupt. This creates a vulnerability that solid state batteries do not share.

A sufficiently powerful or precisely tuned electromagnetic pulse can collapse the coherence state of a quantum battery, causing immediate power loss. For a framework running on quantum batteries, this is catastrophic — not just lost power but potentially destructive discharge as the coherence collapse releases stored energy unpredictably.

Solid state batteries are significantly more resistant to electromagnetic interference. Their operation is electrochemical rather than quantum mechanical and does not depend on maintaining a coherent quantum state.

**Practical implications:**
- EMP weapons in this world are more effective against high-tier augmented individuals than low-tier ones, which inverts the expected logic — better augments, greater vulnerability to a specific attack type
- Military and specialist frameworks using quantum batteries require additional EMP shielding, adding cost and bulk
- Some operators deliberately choose solid state over quantum batteries specifically for EMP resistance, accepting the performance tradeoff for operational security

## Recharge Infrastructure

Both battery types recharge through the same two methods established in the [[Bandwidth & Power]] game systems document:

**Wireless sleep recharge** — the framework draws power from ambient electromagnetic infrastructure during sleep. Requires being within range of charging infrastructure, which in New Halcyon tracks district quality exactly as other infrastructure does. Wealthy districts have dense wireless charging coverage. Forgotten districts have none, or intermittent coverage from degraded infrastructure.

**Wired fast charge** — direct connection through the charging port, significantly faster, requires being stationary and physically connected. The charging port is also a data transfer port — see [[Pairing Protocol & Network Security]] for the security implications of this.
