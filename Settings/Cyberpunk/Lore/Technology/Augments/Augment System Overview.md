# Augmentation — Overview

*What it means to be augmented in New Halcyon. The culture, the class, the cost.*

---

## What Augmentation Is

Augmentation is the installation of hardware into the body via a framework integration layer. It is not unusual in New Halcyon — it is the norm for anyone with the means to access it. Multiple generations have grown up in a city where augmented bodies are unremarkable.

What varies is the quality, the ownership model, and what the augments cost beyond their price tag.

---

## The Framework Standard

Almost all augmentation in New Halcyon runs on SOMA — Standardised Open Modification Architecture. SOMA is open source. No single entity owns it. Any manufacturer can build to the standard without licensing fees.

This means the profit layer sits entirely in the augments, services, and subscriptions built on top of SOMA rather than in the standard itself. The standard is free. What runs on it is not.

A small number of individuals run proprietary frameworks — hardware developed by specific conglomerates for their own personnel. These are not consumer products. They exist for control and operational security reasons. See [[Lore/Technology/Augments/Framework]].

---

## Class and Augmentation

Augmentation in New Halcyon tracks class almost perfectly.

**Conglomerate employees and wealthy residents** receive or can afford high tier frameworks and augments through employment packages or direct purchase. Their augments are current, well installed, and maintained through legitimate channels.

**Middle tier residents** run functional augments, usually tier 2 or 3, often on subscription models that keep them financially tied to manufacturers or conglomerates. They can access licensed independent fitters.

**Poor residents in forgotten districts** access augmentation through unlicensed fitters working with salvage, grey market stock, and improvised installation. The augments function. The installation quality is unpredictable. The debt structure is often predatory.

The physical difference between someone with quality augmentation and someone running poorly installed grey market hardware becomes legible over time — in how they move, how they respond, in the subtle tells of a system running inefficiently inside a body.

---

## Ownership and Debt

Not all augments are owned by the person wearing them.

Subscription augments are the dominant corporate model — the hardware is subsidised or provided, the capability is licensed on an ongoing basis. Missing payments results in degraded function or deactivation. The augment is inside your body and it is not yours.

Conglomerate gifted augments carry their own obligations — loyalty expectations, surveillance capabilities, dependencies that serve the conglomerate's interests as much as the wearer's.

Outright purchase exists but is expensive. It is the preference of those who can afford it and those who understand what they are giving up by not owning their own hardware.

---

## The Unaugmented

Choosing not to augment in New Halcyon is a legitimate position. For some it is poverty — the framework and core stack represent a real financial threshold. For others it is a deliberate refusal — of the debt, the surveillance, the dependency.

Unaugmented individuals are not helpless. The city has always had people who move through it without corporate hardware inside them, and some of those people are very good at what they do precisely because they have had to find other ways.
