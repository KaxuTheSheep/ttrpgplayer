Whenever you attempt something challenging — whether it's climbing a treacherous wall, negotiating with a wary contact, or searching for hidden secrets — you'll roll **two ten-sided dice (2d10)** and add any relevant bonuses or penalties.

# Tests
Tests refer to any 2d10 Roll:
- **Roll 2d10 and add your modifiers.**
- The result will be compared to a Difficulty Number (DN) set by the Game Master (GM).
- **If your total is equal to or higher than the DN, you succeed.**
- If it's lower, your [[Actions|action]] fails or has unintended consequences.

## Advantage and Disadvantage
Sometimes, circumstances make your task easier or harder. When that happens, you might gain **Advantage** or suffer from **Disadvantage**. You may multiple sources of **Advantage** and **Disadvantage** at the same time, each of which adds a single d10 to your roll. For every advantage, you ignore 1 die with the lowest value. For every disadvantage, you ignore 1 die with the highest value.

This shifts your chances slightly, giving you a better or worse shot without swinging wildly.

## Critical Success and Critical Failure
Rarely, your roll will be exceptional — either incredibly good or devastatingly bad.

- **Critical Success:** You roll **double 10s** (both dice show 10). This means you have performed your [[Actions|action]] in the best possible way. The GM will often grant extra benefits or bonuses.
- **Critical Failure:** You roll **double 1s** (both dice show 1). This means something has gone terribly wrong, and the GM may introduce complications or setbacks.

## Difficulty Numbers (DNs)
The GM sets a Difficulty Number based on how challenging the task is:

| **Difficulty** | **DN** |
|---|---|
| Very Easy | 5 |
| Easy | 8 |
| Moderate | 12 |
| Hard | 15 |
| Very Hard | 18 |
| Nearly Impossible | 20 |

**Modifiers** are additions to your rolls. They can come from [[Attributes]], [[Skills]], and equipment or abilities as defined by the relevant module.

## Die Steps
A die step increases or decreases the value of a die by 2.
**d2 → d4 → d6 → d8 → d10 → d12 → d14 → d16...**

Any die size is valid — a d7 increased by one step becomes a d9. Die steps stack — two steps on a d6 produces a d10. d2 is the floor; a die cannot be reduced below d2.
