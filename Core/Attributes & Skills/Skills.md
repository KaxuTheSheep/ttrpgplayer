Skills are specific areas of training and expertise built upon your natural Attributes. While your Attributes show what you're generally capable of, Skills represent where you've focused your efforts and experience. When you attempt an action tied to a particular skill, add both your Attribute and your Skill Bonus to your 2d10 roll. This reflects how specialised knowledge sharpens natural talent.

## Training Levels & Skill Bonus
Skill training is purchased using Trait Points from your shared pool. Each level of training in a skill grants a +1 bonus when using that skill. You can spread training broadly for small bonuses in many skills or specialise for higher bonuses in fewer skills.

| Training Level | Description | Skill Bonus |
|---|---|---|
| 0 | Untrained | +0 |
| 1 | Trained | +1 |
| 2 | Skilled | +2 |
| 3 | Expert | +3 |

Each level of training costs 1 Trait Point. Training is capped at your highest linked Attribute — a character cannot exceed a Skill training level higher than their linked Attribute, regardless of points spent.

You may also gain initial skill training from your origin or background as defined in the relevant module. If you gain training in a skill you already have, increase its training level by +1 up to the cap. You can improve skills over time through story milestones, unique opportunities, or other significant narrative events.

## Using Skills
When the GM calls for a skill Test:

1. Roll 2d10.
2. Add your Attribute linked to that skill.
3. Add your Skill Bonus based on training level.
4. Compare the total to the Difficulty Number (DN) set by the GM.

Success means you performed the task with trained expertise; failure means you didn't.

## Skill List
Skills cover niche areas rather than broad Attribute use cases. Modules may add additional skills suited to their setting.

| Skill | Attribute | Description |
| ----- | --------- | ----------- |
| Athletics | Might | Perform physical feats like climbing, swimming, running, and jumping. |
| Crafting | Grace or Insight | Create, repair, or modify tools, weapons, and items. |
| Deft Hands | Grace | Perform delicate or precise tasks, such as fine manipulation. |
| Endurance | Vitality | Resist poison, fatigue, disease, and harsh environments. |
| History | Insight | Recall knowledge of past events, legends, and cultures. |
| Intimidation | Might or Presence | Threaten with physical force or wield authority, reputation, or menace. |
| Intuition | Insight or Resolve | Sense motives, detect lies, and anticipate danger. |
| Investigation | Insight | Search for clues, interpret evidence, and uncover hidden information. |
| Medicine | Insight | Diagnose illness and treat wounds. |
| Negotiation | Presence or Resolve | Reach agreements, bargains, and compromises through lie or truth. |
| Perception | Insight | Become aware of something through the senses. |
| Performance | Presence | Entertain through music, acting, or storytelling. |
| Stealth | Grace or Insight | Move quietly and avoid detection, or exploit shadows and distractions. |
| Survival | Insight or Resolve | Track creatures, find food, and endure hostile environments. |
