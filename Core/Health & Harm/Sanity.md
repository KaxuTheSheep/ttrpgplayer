Sanity represents your character's mental steadiness — the ability to remain focused, grounded, and emotionally composed under stress. Where [[Blood]] measures the body's life force, Sanity measures the mind's resilience. As Sanity is depleted, the mind begins to fray, and stress reactions, panic responses, or deeper psychological disturbances may surface.

## Calculation
**Sanity = Resolve + Origin Bonus + Level Bonus + Other Modifiers**

- **Resolve:** Core mental strength and determination.
- **Origin Bonus:** Some backgrounds or paths cultivate discipline, reflection, or mental conditioning. Defined in the relevant module.
- **Level Bonus:** Each level up grants +1 to your maximum Sanity, reflecting the hardened mind of a veteran.
- **Other Modifiers:** Can arise from training, abilities, or rare mental conditioning.

## Function
Sanity is reduced by fear, trauma, psychological strain, and certain encounters as defined by the relevant module. Losing Sanity does not represent physical injury — it represents stress load building against the mind. When Sanity reaches critically low levels, the character is at risk of developing [[Afflictions]], lasting mental scars that shape outlook, behaviour, or emotional response.

## Sanity Thresholds

| Current Sanity | State | Mechanical Effect |
|---|---|---|
| ≥75% | Steady | No mechanical penalties. |
| 50–74% | Uneasy | Disadvantage on Intuition Tests. |
| 25–49% | Strained | Disadvantage on Negotiation Tests. |
| 10–24% | Fracturing | Once per encounter, when choosing to hold position in combat, make a Resolve Test. |
| 1–9% | Breaking Point | Before each Rest, make a Resolve Test vs DN set by the GM. On a failed Test, gain an Affliction. |
| 0 | Break | Gain 1 Affliction, then restore Sanity to 1. |

## Sanity Dice
Sanity Dice represent your mind's natural capacity to recover during rest. They start at **d4** and may increase through progression as defined in the relevant module or origin system. Spent dice are regained on a Long Rest.
