Afflictions represent disruptions to the **mind** rather than the body. They reflect stress responses, deep-seated fears, supernatural influence, and psychological strain. Unlike physical [[Injuries|Wounds]], Afflictions influence **thoughts, behaviour, perception, or emotional reactions**.

Afflictions are **specific** in nature. Two characters may both be stressed, but one may shake uncontrollably near blood, while another becomes numb or withdrawn.

Afflictions do **not** impose broad universal penalties like exhaustion. Instead, each Affliction defines when it applies and what effect it has.

## Gaining Afflictions
Afflictions arise when a character experiences **severe mental strain**, psychological shock, or supernatural influence. They do not occur randomly — they appear when the character reaches a point of **mental vulnerability**.

Afflictions are gained in two primary ways:

### 1. Sanity Threshold Break
When **Sanity** falls to a **critical threshold**, the mind is at risk.
- The **player rolls a Resolve Test** before each rest.
- **On success:** The character remains composed. No Affliction gained.
- **On failure:** The character gains an **Affliction**, reflecting how their mind breaks under pressure.
- At 0 Sanity, the player automatically gains an **Affliction** and goes to 1 Sanity.

### 2. Narrative or Supernatural Triggers
Afflictions may also be gained when a character is exposed to intense:
- **Fear** (e.g., witnessing gore, confronting a nightmare creature)
- **Trauma** (e.g., losing an ally, surviving a near-death event)
- **Supernatural Influence** (reality distortion, void effects, or similar forces defined by the relevant module)
- **Ability Strain** (overcasting, forbidden techniques, or similar as defined by the relevant module)

If the cause specifies that it **imposes an Affliction:**
- The GM may provide a **list of suitable Afflictions**, or
- A **specific Affliction** if the source is thematic.

## Player Choice and GM Guidance
When an Affliction is gained from Sanity Thresholds:
- The **player proposes an Affliction** that reflects how **their character personally reacts** to the experience.
- The **GM approves** the choice, ensuring it fits the tone, situation, and severity.

This ensures Afflictions **express character**, not just mechanics.

However, when the Affliction is caused by spells, supernatural entities, or module-specific sources, the **GM** may provide a specific Affliction or a restricted list appropriate to that source.

### Affliction Severity
Each Affliction has three levels. A character may progress from Minor → Moderate → Severe if the stress is repeated or unresolved.

| Severity | Mechanical Effect | Emotional Impact | Recovery |
| -------- | ---------------- | ---------------- | -------- |
| **Minor** | Small, situational penalty | Moment of fear, stress, or instability | **Break / Short Rest** may remove the Affliction |
| **Moderate** | Noticeable behavioural influence or disadvantage in specific situations | The reaction becomes a pattern | **Long Rest** may remove the Affliction |
| **Severe** | Strong psychological disruption; automatic behaviours or intrusive states | The Affliction shapes identity or daily function | Recovery requires **roleplay arc, ritual, or therapy** |

## Hemophobia
_Fear or aversion to fresh blood._
- **Minor:** When exposed to **fresh blood**, you hesitate. For 1 round, you take **–1** to **physical tests** involving violence.
- **Moderate:** When **seeing or smelling fresh blood**, make a **Resolve Test (DN 12)**. **On failure:** You **freeze** for **1 Round** (cannot take Actions, but may move).
- **Severe:** You **cannot willingly cause bleeding or harm** a creature without a **Resolve Test (DN 16)**. **On failure:** You must **Withdraw**, retreat, or avert your gaze, and **freeze** if unable to.

## Hypervigilance
_Your senses remain keyed for threat. Your mind expects danger even in safety._
- **Minor:** When entering a **new room, hallway, clearing, or environment**, you must **Stop and Scan** before proceeding. This costs **1 Half Action** (in combat) or **a brief pause** (out of combat).
- **Moderate:** When attempting to **Rest** or relax, make a **Resolve Test (DN 12)**. **On failure:** You cannot relax and **do not restore Sanity** from that rest.
- **Severe:** During **Long Rest**, your mind resists standing down. Each day of the Long Rest, make a **Resolve Test (DN 14)**. **On success:** Restore **10% of your maximum Sanity**. **On failure:** Lose **5% of your current Sanity** (minimum reduction of 1).

## Emotional Numbing
_Feelings are distant; expression becomes difficult or forced._
- **Minor:** You take **Disadvantage** on **Performance** and **Negotiation** when emotions are involved.
- **Moderate:** When attempting to **express or act on emotion** (comforting, confessing, rallying morale), make a **Resolve Test (DN 12)**. **On failure:** You **cannot complete the emotional action** and instead remain flat or silent.
- **Severe:** **Morale, empathy, or emotional support abilities cannot affect you or be spread by you** unless you first pass a **Resolve Test (DN 14)**.

## Intrusive Memory Loops
_Flashbacks or repeating memories override the present moment._
- **Minor:** When a **memory trigger** occurs (sound, smell, location), you lose **your Half Action** next Round.
- **Moderate:** When a **memory trigger** occurs, make a **Resolve Test (DN 12)**. **On failure:** You **freeze for 1 round**, reliving past and present at once.
- **Severe:** When triggered, make a **Resolve Test (DN 14)**. **On failure:** You freeze and **misinterpret the scene**, treating familiar allies or objects as elements of the memory for the duration.

## Avoidance Response
_Your body instinctively pulls you away from distress rather than through it._
- **Minor:** When confronted with **personal emotional stakes** or **intimate conflict**, you take **–1** to **Negotiation** and **Performance** tests.
- **Moderate:** When directly **confronted** (emotionally or physically), make a **Resolve Test (DN 12)**. **On failure:** You **Withdraw** (move away) and cannot **Attack** this round.
- **Severe:** When initiating **confrontation** (verbal OR violent), make a **Resolve Test (DN 16)**. **On failure:** You must **retreat or freeze** if escape is blocked.

## Social Withdrawal
_Being near others feels overwhelming, exposing, or unsafe._
- **Minor:** When in groups of **3 or more**, take **–1** to **Negotiation** and **Performance** checks.
- **Moderate:** When someone attempts to **emotionally engage** (comfort, connect, question motives), make a **Resolve Test (DN 12)**. **On failure:** You **freeze for 1 Round** and cannot speak beyond short, minimal responses.
- **Severe:** You cannot **willingly initiate or sustain conversation** without a **Resolve Test (DN 14)**. **On failure:** You **withdraw** from the interaction or physically move away.

## Irritability Surge
_Your body prepares for conflict faster than your mind does._
- **Minor:** When a creature **interrupts**, challenges, obstructs, or provokes you (verbally or physically), your attention **locks onto that creature**. Until the creature is no longer present or the conflict ends, any Actions you take that could meaningfully target a creature **must target that creature**. You can still Move, Withdraw, Block, Speak, or perform neutral actions.
- **Moderate:** When your action is obstructed or challenged, make a **Resolve Test (DN 12)**. **On failure:** You **snap**, raising your voice or posture; you **cannot Help** or cooperate this round.
- **Severe:** When provoked, confronted, or insulted, make a **Resolve Test (DN 14)**. **On failure:** You **escalate** instinctively — either stepping forward, issuing a threat, or placing a hand on a weapon. _(This does not force violence — it forces tension.)_

## Panic Spike
_Your body reacts faster than conscious thought when fear increases._
- **Minor:** When you are **surprised** or a threat emerges unexpectedly, you **lose your Half Action** next round.
- **Moderate:** When **ambushed, surrounded, or overwhelmed**, make a **Resolve Test (DN 12)**. **On failure:** You **freeze for 1 round**.
- **Severe:** When faced with overwhelming threat, make a **Resolve Test (DN 14)**. **On failure:** You must **Withdraw** immediately and attempt to increase distance from the threat source. If unable, you **freeze** instead.

## Perfection Compulsion
_If everything is not correct, safety feels impossible._
- **Minor:** When you start your Rest, one of your Rest Activities has to be Repairing your Gear.
- **Moderate:** When performing a **precision action** (aimed attack, disarming traps, lockpicking, careful spellcasting, medical treatment, crafting, or manipulating gear), your **Action's Speed is reduced by 1**.
- **Severe:** When attempting any task where **failing would have serious consequences**, make a **Resolve Test (DN 14)**. **On failure:** You **cannot initiate the action this round** — you hesitate, re-check, or recalculate. You may still Move or Withdraw. **On success:** You may act normally, but your Speed penalty from Moderate still applies.

## Attachment Fragility
_When you care about someone, it feels dangerous._
- **Minor:** When acting to **support, protect, or assist** someone you care about, you must **Act immediately**, but take **–1** to the roll.
- **Moderate:** When an ally you care about is **injured**, make a **Resolve Test (DN 14)**. **On failure:** You **freeze for 1 round** as fear overrides action.
- **Severe:** You cannot **willingly place yourself in danger to defend someone you care about** without a **Resolve Test (DN 16)**. **On failure:** You **hesitate** and may only **Move** or **Withdraw** that round.
