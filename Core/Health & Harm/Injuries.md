Injuries represent lasting harm beyond simple [[Blood]] loss. They take the form of **wounds**, each imposing penalties and requiring treatment. Left untreated, wounds can worsen or even become permanent.

### How to Obtain Injuries
Characters can suffer **wounds** or **permanent injuries** through severe harm.

- **Critical Successes:**  
    When you are hit by a **Critical Success** on an **[[Attacks|Attack]]**, you suffer **1 wound**.  
    Each wound imposes a **–1 penalty** to **Physical Tests** (tests involving **[[Attributes|Might]]**, **[[Attributes|Grace]]**, or **[[Attributes|Vitality]]**).

- **Critical Lethality:**  
    If a **Critical Success** reduces your **[[Blood]]** to **0 or below**, you suffer a **permanent injury** instead of a normal wound.  
    A permanent injury:
    - Begins as a wound (and must be **closed** to stop active impairment),
    - But also imposes lasting limitations until treated through advanced means.

### Treating Wounds
A wound can be **closed** to stop its penalty, but closed wounds can still reopen later.
You can close a wound by:
- Succeeding a **[[Skills|Medicine]] Test (DN 14)**.
- Using **healing items**, which automatically close wounds.
- Receiving **healing effects**, which close wounds but do _not_ restore [[Blood]].

> Closing a wound removes its penalty, but does not permanently heal it — closed wounds remain at risk of reopening until fully healed.

### Wounds Reopening
When a character's [[Blood]] drops to a low threshold, closed wounds may reopen, which acts as a normal open wound. Permanent injuries remain in effect, but their associated wounds can still reopen if [[Blood]] is critically low.

### Healing Wounds Permanently
Closed wounds heal completely after a **long [[Rest & Recovery|rest]]** without incident. Once healed, they cannot reopen.  
Some abilities or effects can also remove a wound instantly, regardless of its state.

### Permanent Injuries
When a critical hit reduces your [[Blood]] to **0 or below**, it inflicts a **permanent injury** — severe trauma such as lost limbs, internal [[Damage Types|damage]], or lasting impairment.

Permanent injuries:
- **Function as wounds** in terms of bleeding or immediate penalties and must be **closed** to stop ongoing harm.
- Closing a permanent injury wound (e.g., bandaging a severed arm) **stops the active bleeding or immediate [[Damage Types|damage]]**, but the wound can **reopen** if [[Blood]] drops too low again or the injury is aggravated.
- Even after a wound is healed, **lasting mechanical and narrative effects remain**.
    - Example: You lost an arm. It has been healed so there is no wound left, however you still do not have an arm, so any function that requires it will be impossible.

**Permanent Injury Mechanics:**
- Closing the wound requires a **[[Skills|Medicine]] Test (DN 18)** or equivalent healing.
- Full reversal or restoration of the injury (removing the lingering limitation) requires advanced means as defined by the relevant module.

### Permanent Injury List
Each permanent injury carries both **narrative** and **mechanical** consequences. These effects persist even after the wound is closed, unless removed through advanced means such as prosthetics, expert medical reconstruction, or module-specific treatments.

| **Injury** | **Description** | **Lasting Limitation** |
| ---------- | --------------- | ---------------------- |
| **Lost Arm** | A full arm is destroyed, amputated, or rendered unusable. | Cannot wield two-handed weapons or carry a shield in that arm. Disadvantage on tasks requiring two hands (e.g., climbing, heavy lifting). |
| **Lost Leg** | One leg is lost or crushed beyond recovery. | [[Movement Types|Movement]] Distance halved. Disadvantage on checks involving balance, running, or jumping. Requires support (crutch/prosthetic) to move effectively. |
| **Damaged Eye** | One eye is blinded or severely injured. | –2 to [[Evasion]] and Disadvantage on ranged [[Attacks|attacks]] or [[Skills|perception]] checks involving sight. |
| **Crushed Spine** | Severe spinal trauma impairs full mobility. | Cannot Run or Jump. [[Movement Types|Movement]] Distance reduced by 75%. May require assistance to move at all. |
| **Mangled Hand** | Fingers or hand destroyed beyond use. | Cannot wield weapons or hold objects with that hand. Disadvantage on precision or tool-related checks. |
| **Punctured Lung** | Deep injury impairs breathing. | Stamina-related checks suffer Disadvantage. Max [[Sturdiness]] reduced by 2. |
| **Shattered Jaw** | Jaw crushed or broken, difficult to speak or eat. | Speech-based checks at Disadvantage. |
| **Fractured Skull** | Traumatic head injury causes chronic disorientation. | Disadvantage on [[Attributes|Insight]] Tests. Disadvantage on Tests to resist Stunned or Dazed [[Conditions|conditions]]. |
| **Severed Ear** | Loss of hearing in one or both ears. | Disadvantage on [[Skills|Perception]] checks relying on sound. Cannot be surprised by audible cues. |
| **Internal Organ [[Damage Types|Damage]]** | Critical blow damages vital organs. | Max [[Blood]] reduced by 2 permanently. Healing effects restore half their normal amount. |
| **Burn Scarring** | Severe burns limit mobility and cause chronic pain. | Disadvantage on [[Attributes|Grace]]-based Tests. Cannot wear heavy armour comfortably. |
| **Nerve [[Damage Types|Damage]]** | Permanent numbness or pain in limbs. | Disadvantage on [[Attributes|Grace]]- or [[Attributes|Might]]-based [[Skills|Skill]] Tests involving fine control. |
| **Lost Eye (Both)** | Total blindness from dual eye loss. | Permanently Blinded [[Conditions|condition]]. Must rely on other senses or assistance. |
