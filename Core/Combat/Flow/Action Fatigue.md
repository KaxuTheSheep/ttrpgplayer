Repeating the same special ability across [[Rounds]] causes it to become less effective. This represents technique predictability, diminishing momentum, and accumulated exertion. A fighter who relies on the same move repeatedly becomes easier to read and counter.

**Special abilities** are biological traits, trained techniques, magical effects, augmentations, or similar — anything beyond basic [[Weapon|weapon]] use. Standard [[Attacks|attacks]] with a [[Weapon|weapon]] or basic item use are fluid and naturally chain together regardless of repetition. [[Actions|Action]] Fatigue does not apply to them.

## How It Works
Each time you use the same special ability in consecutive [[Rounds]], it accumulates a **–2 penalty** to its roll.

At the end of each [[Rounds|Round]], every tracked penalty reduces by **1**, reflecting partial recovery and flow adjustment.

**Example:** A character uses the same dash [[Attacks|attack]] three [[Rounds]] in a row.

- [[Rounds|Round]] 1: No penalty.
- [[Rounds|Round]] 2: –2 to the roll.
- [[Rounds|Round]] 3: –3 to the roll (–2 new repeat, –1 carried from last [[Rounds|round]] after decay).

If they switch to a different ability in [[Rounds|Round]] 4, the penalty on the dash [[Attacks|attack]] continues to decay by 1 each [[Rounds|Round]] until it reaches 0.
