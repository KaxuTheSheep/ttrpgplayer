Repeating the same special ability across Rounds causes it to become less effective. This represents technique predictability, diminishing momentum, and accumulated exertion. A fighter who relies on the same move repeatedly becomes easier to read and counter.

**Special abilities** are biological traits, trained techniques, magical effects, augmentations, or similar — anything beyond basic weapon use. Standard attacks with a weapon or basic item use are fluid and naturally chain together regardless of repetition. Action Fatigue does not apply to them.

## How It Works
Each time you use the same special ability in consecutive Rounds, it accumulates a **–2 penalty** to its roll.

At the end of each Round, every tracked penalty reduces by **1**, reflecting partial recovery and flow adjustment.

**Example:** A character uses the same dash attack three Rounds in a row.

- Round 1: No penalty.
- Round 2: –2 to the roll.
- Round 3: –3 to the roll (–2 new repeat, –1 carried from last round after decay).

If they switch to a different ability in Round 4, the penalty on the dash attack continues to decay by 1 each Round until it reaches 0.
