### Ticks, Priority & Resolution
**Priority** determines **when** an [[Actions|action]] happens in the [[Rounds|round]].  
All actions and events flow on a timeline called Ticks, similar to game ticks in video games. 
All [[Actions|actions]], techniques, and abilities list a **Priority**, this is the value for which tick it'll end up on, some abilities or movement occur over time meaning once activated at a priority they take a duration of ticks to complete to the set or desired amount of ticks.
During combat:
- **All combatants declare their [[Actions|actions]] first**
- Then [[Actions|actions]] **resolve in descending Priority order**
- Any [[Actions|actions]] with the **same Priority** resolve **simultaneously**

#### Tie-breakers
If two or more [[Actions|actions]] share the same Priority:
1. The character with the **higher [[Attributes|Grace]] score** resolves first.
2. If [[Attributes|Grace]] also ties, the GM decides the order narratively.

### Staggered Priority (Voluntary Delay)
You may **lower the Priority** of one of your declared [[Actions|actions]] to match an ally or wait for an enemy's commitment.
You **cannot increase** a Priority above its listed base.  
You may only **lower** it.
This allows:
- Coordinating team strikes
- Counter-timing
- Baiting an enemy's [[Movement Types|movement]] or defence

There is **no penalty** for staggering Priority.

### Simultaneous Resolution
If two [[Actions|actions]] occur at the **same Priority**, they both happen **even if one would harm or kill the other**.

Example:  
Two fighters both strike at Priority 11 → **both [[Attacks|attacks]] land** before [[Damage Types|damage]] is applied and effects are processed.

This creates:
- Cinematic exchanges
- True risk when fighting aggressively
- Real possibility of mutual [[Injuries|injury]]

### Reactive Abilities
Some abilities trigger **in response** to another [[Actions|action]] rather than being declared up front.  
If a reactive ability has a **Priority**, compare its Priority to the triggering [[Actions|action]]:
- If the **Reactive Priority is higher**, it interrupts and resolves first but in the same tick as the action.
- If **lower**, the triggering [[Actions|action]] resolves first, if the reaction allows it, it can then resolve afterwards at the original priority.

Example:
- Quick Reflexes (Priority 14) vs a sword swing (Priority 10) → **you move before the swing lands in the same Tick of 10**.
- If the sword swing were Priority 16 → **you move too late and if you want you can keep the reaction occurring after the attack**.
