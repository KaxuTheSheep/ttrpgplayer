### Speed & Resolution
**Speed** determines **when** an [[Actions|action]] happens in the [[Rounds|round]].  
A **higher Speed value = faster [[Actions|action]]** (resolves earlier).  
A **lower Speed value = slower [[Actions|action]]** (resolves later).
All [[Actions|actions]], movements, techniques, and abilities list a **Speed value**.
During combat:
- **All combatants declare their [[Actions|actions]] first**
- Then [[Actions|actions]] **[[Attributes|resolve]] in descending Speed order**
- Any [[Actions|actions]] with the **same Speed** [[Attributes|resolve]] **simultaneously**

#### Tie-breakers
If two or more [[Actions|actions]] share the same Speed:
1. The character with the **higher [[Attributes|Grace]] score** resolves first.
2. If [[Attributes|Grace]] also ties, the GM decides the order narratively.

### Staggered Speed (Voluntary Delay)
You may **lower the Speed** of one of your declared [[Actions|actions]] to match an ally or wait for an enemy's commitment.
You **cannot increase** a Speed above its listed base.  
You may only **lower** it.
This allows:
- Coordinating team strikes
- Counter-timing
- Baiting an enemy's [[Movement Types|movement]] or defence

There is **no penalty** for staggering speed.

### Simultaneous Resolution
If two [[Actions|actions]] occur at the **same Speed**, they both happen **even if one would harm or kill the other**.

Example:  
Two fighters both strike at Speed 11 → **both [[Attacks|attacks]] land** before [[Damage Types|damage]] is applied and effects are processed.

This creates:
- Cinematic exchanges
- True risk when fighting aggressively
- Real possibility of mutual [[Injuries|injury]]

### Reactive Abilities
Some abilities trigger **in response** to another [[Actions|action]] rather than being declared up front.  
If a reactive ability has a **Speed**, compare its Speed to the triggering [[Actions|action]]:
- If the **Reactive Speed is higher**, it interrupts and resolves first.
- If **lower**, the triggering [[Actions|action]] resolves first.

Example:
- Quick Reflexes (Speed 14) vs a sword swing (Speed 10) → **you move before the swing lands**.
- If the sword swing were Speed 16 → **you move too late**.
