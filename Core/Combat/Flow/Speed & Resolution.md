### Speed & Resolution
**Speed** determines **when** an action happens in the round.  
A **higher Speed value = faster action** (resolves earlier).  
A **lower Speed value = slower action** (resolves later).
All actions, movements, techniques, and abilities list a **Speed value**.
During combat:
- **All combatants declare their actions first**
- Then actions **resolve in descending Speed order**
- Any actions with the **same Speed** resolve **simultaneously**

#### Tie-breakers
If two or more actions share the same Speed:
1. The character with the **higher Grace score** resolves first.
2. If Grace also ties, the GM decides the order narratively.

### Staggered Speed (Voluntary Delay)
You may **lower the Speed** of one of your declared actions to match an ally or wait for an enemy's commitment.
You **cannot increase** a Speed above its listed base.  
You may only **lower** it.
This allows:
- Coordinating team strikes
- Counter-timing
- Baiting an enemy's movement or defence

There is **no penalty** for staggering speed.

### Simultaneous Resolution
If two actions occur at the **same Speed**, they both happen **even if one would harm or kill the other**.

Example:  
Two fighters both strike at Speed 11 → **both attacks land** before damage is applied and effects are processed.

This creates:
- Cinematic exchanges
- True risk when fighting aggressively
- Real possibility of mutual injury

### Reactive Abilities
Some abilities trigger **in response** to another action rather than being declared up front.  
If a reactive ability has a **Speed**, compare its Speed to the triggering action:
- If the **Reactive Speed is higher**, it interrupts and resolves first.
- If **lower**, the triggering action resolves first.

Example:
- Quick Reflexes (Speed 14) vs a sword swing (Speed 10) → **you move before the swing lands**.
- If the sword swing were Speed 16 → **you move too late**.
