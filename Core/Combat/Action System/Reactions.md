Reactions are responses taken during the Resolution Phase, allowing a character to defend, reposition, or interrupt when something happens to them. They are not declared at the start of the Round.

- You may take one Reaction per round.
- A Reaction can only be used if its conditions are met.
- A Reaction happens at the Speed of the action or movement associated with it, unless stated otherwise.

## Common Triggers
- You are targeted by an attack.
- An enemy moves into your melee threat.
- You are affected by an effect that allows defensive timing.
- A ranged projectile is in flight toward you.

| Reaction | Trigger | Effect |
| -------- | ------- | ------ |
| Dodge (Reaction use) | You are targeted by any attack requiring a roll | Avoid the attack if your Evasion is not overcome |
| Reach Punish | An enemy enters your melee threat | Make a single attack against them as they enter |
| Turn to Face | You need to rotate during Resolution | Spend your Reaction to change facing immediately |
| Movement Interrupt | You are currently moving during Resolution | Immediately change your movement direction, switch to a different target, or stop entirely at your current position |
| Use Cover | A ranged projectile is in flight toward you | Use your reaction and movement to get to cover. Speed is used to determine if the player gets there in time and normal range rules apply. |

## Movement Reactions
During the Resolution Phase, if you are mid-movement you may spend your Reaction to:
- Change your movement direction entirely.
- Switch to a different destination or target.
- Stop at your current position along your declared path.

This represents a character reacting to unfolding events — ducking behind cover when an arrow is loosed, redirecting toward a new threat, or halting when a path becomes blocked. The Reaction resolves at the Speed of your current movement.

## Restrictions
You cannot use a Reaction if:
- You have already used a Reaction this round.
- You are Stunned, Immobilised, or otherwise prevented from acting.
