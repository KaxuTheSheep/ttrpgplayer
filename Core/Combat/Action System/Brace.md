Brace is a defensive action that allows a character to reset their guard, recover their posture, and restore lost [[Sturdiness]] mid-combat. It represents a fighter tightening their stance, taking a brief reprieve after a successful deflection, or finding a moment of composure under pressure.

## Action Cost
Brace can be performed at two levels depending on how much time you commit.

| Version | Action Cost | Speed | Recovery |
|---|---|---|---|
| Quick Brace | Half Action | 8 | 1d4 ÷ 2 rounded up (1–2 Sturdiness) |
| Full Brace | Full Action | 8 | 1d4 (1–4 Sturdiness) |

## Timing
Brace recovery resolves at Speed 8. If an attack applies damage to your Sturdiness before Speed 8 is reached, Brace is cancelled and no recovery occurs. If the attack resolves after Speed 8, the Sturdiness is recovered first and the incoming damage is then applied to the restored pool.

Damage that is fully absorbed by Armour DR before reaching Sturdiness does not cancel Brace.

## How It Works in Practice
Brace rewards characters who build around their defences. A fighter who successfully zeroes out incoming damage through Block, Armour, or Parry can Brace freely. A nimble character who Dodges or Parries incoming attacks creates the opening needed.

> **Example:** A shield fighter declares Full Brace and Block in the same round. An attack hits at Speed 12 — before Brace resolves at Speed 8. Block reduces the damage to 0 via Armour DR. Since no damage reached Sturdiness, Brace completes and the fighter recovers 1d4 Sturdiness.

> **Example:** A fighter declares Quick Brace. An attack hits at Speed 6 — after Brace resolves at Speed 8. The fighter recovers Sturdiness first, then takes the hit against the restored pool.
