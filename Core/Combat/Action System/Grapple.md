Grappling is a body-based method of controlling another creature. It represents seizing, restraining, and manipulating a target through physical force rather than weapons. While Grappling, the initiator can drag, reposition, and set up follow-up actions, with deeper manipulation options unlocked through Discipline abilities.

## Initiating a Grapple
**Action Cost:** Full Action
**Roll:** 2d10 + Might or Grace vs target's Evasion

If the attack roll meets or exceeds the target's Evasion, the initiator has reached the target. Resolve the grapple attempt:

**Opposed Roll:** Initiator's Might or Grace vs target's Might or Grace
- If the initiator wins, the target gains the [[Conditions#Grappled|Grappled]] condition and the initiator enters the Grappling state.
- If the target wins, the attempt fails and no condition is applied.

## Maintaining a Grapple
Maintaining a Grapple costs a **Full Action** each round. If the initiator does not spend this action, the Grapple ends immediately and the Grappled condition is removed from the target.

## Grappling State
While Grappling, the initiator:
- May move, dragging the target with them. Walk rate is halved. Cannot Run.
- Cannot use Reach or Cumbersome weapons — attacks with these weapons are at Disadvantage while maintaining a Grapple.

## Escaping a Grapple
The Grappled target may attempt to escape as a **Full Action**:

**Opposed Roll:** Target's Might or Grace vs initiator's Might or Grace
- On success, the Grappled condition is removed and the Grappling state ends.
- On failure, the Grapple is maintained.

## Ending a Grapple
A Grapple ends when:
- The initiator does not spend their Full Action to maintain it.
- The target succeeds an escape attempt.
- The initiator is Stunned, Prone, or otherwise incapacitated.
- The initiator voluntarily releases the target.
