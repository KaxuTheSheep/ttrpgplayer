Grappling is a body-based method of controlling another creature. It represents seizing, restraining, and manipulating a target through physical force rather than weapons. While Grappling, the initiator can drag, reposition, and set up follow-up [[actions]], with deeper manipulation options unlocked through Discipline abilities.

## Initiating a Grapple
**[[Actions|Action]] Cost:** Full [[Actions|Action]]
**Roll:** 2d10 + [[Attributes|Might]] or [[Attributes|Grace]] vs target's [[Evasion]]

If the [[Attacks|attack]] roll meets or exceeds the target's [[Evasion]], the initiator has reached the target. [[Attributes|Resolve]] the grapple attempt:

**Opposed Roll:** Initiator's [[Attributes|Might]] or [[Attributes|Grace]] vs target's [[Attributes|Might]] or [[Attributes|Grace]]
- If the initiator wins, the target gains the [[Conditions#Grappled|Grappled]] [[Conditions|condition]] and the initiator enters the Grappling state.
- If the target wins, the attempt fails and no [[Conditions|condition]] is applied.

## Maintaining a Grapple
Maintaining a Grapple costs a **Full [[Actions|Action]]** each [[Rounds|round]]. If the initiator does not spend this [[Actions|action]], the Grapple ends immediately and the Grappled [[Conditions|condition]] is removed from the target.

## Grappling State
While Grappling, the initiator:
- May move, dragging the target with them. Walk rate is halved. Cannot Run.
- Cannot use Reach or Cumbersome weapons — [[attacks]] with these weapons are at Disadvantage while maintaining a Grapple.

## Escaping a Grapple
The Grappled target may attempt to escape as a **Full [[Actions|Action]]**:

**Opposed Roll:** Target's [[Attributes|Might]] or [[Attributes|Grace]] vs initiator's [[Attributes|Might]] or [[Attributes|Grace]]
- On success, the Grappled [[Conditions|condition]] is removed and the Grappling state ends.
- On failure, the Grapple is maintained.

## Ending a Grapple
A Grapple ends when:
- The initiator does not spend their Full [[Actions|Action]] to maintain it.
- The target succeeds an escape attempt.
- The initiator is Stunned, Prone, or otherwise incapacitated.
- The initiator voluntarily releases the target.
