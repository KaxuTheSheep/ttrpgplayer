### Off-Hand Strikes
If you are wielding a **Light** [[Weapon|weapon]] in your off-hand:
- When you make a **melee [[Attacks|Attack]]** with your main-hand [[Weapon|weapon]], you may make **one Off-Hand Strike** as part of the **same Full [[Actions|Action]]**.
- The **Off-Hand Strike** uses **only the [[Weapon|weapon]]'s [[Damage Types|Damage]] Die** (no [[Attributes|Attribute]]).
- The **Off-Hand Strike resolves at**:
  **Final [[Speed & Resolution|Speed]] = Off-Hand [[Weapon]] Base [[Speed & Resolution|Speed]] + [[Attributes|Attribute]] – 2**

This represents the slight inefficiency of coordinating two weapons without making it weak.

#### Defensive Benefit
If you are wielding **two weapons** and declare **[[Parry]]**, you gain:
- **+1 to [[Parry]] Tests**

#### Restrictions
- The off-hand [[Weapon|weapon]] must be **Light**.
- Only **one** Off-Hand Strike per **[[Rounds|Round]]**, regardless of how many [[Attacks]] you take.

#### Buckler Compatibility
Bucklers can be strapped to the forearm, allowing use alongside Two-Handed, Versatile, and Dual Wield loadouts without penalty to either the Buckler or the [[Weapon|weapon]].

#### [[Action Fatigue]]
Off-Hand Strikes do not trigger or accumulate [[Action Fatigue]] independently. They share the fatigue of the main hand [[Attacks|attack]] and are not tracked separately.

#### Versatile Weapons
A Versatile [[Weapon|weapon]] wielded alongside an off-hand [[Weapon|weapon]] is considered One-Handed and does not gain the [[Damage Types|damage]] die step. Both hands must be free to grip the [[Weapon|weapon]] for the Two-Handed bonus to apply.
