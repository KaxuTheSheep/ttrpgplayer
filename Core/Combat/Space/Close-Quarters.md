## Close-Quarters & Engagement States

Melee combat is not only about _whether_ you can reach someone, but **how** your weapon interacts at different distances. The distance between combatants determines **control**, **leverage**, and **defensive options**.

### Core Distances
There are **three engagement ranges**:

| Range | Description | Tactical Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| **Outside Threat** | Neither combatant can reach | No melee attacks possible. |
| **Threat Range** | Both combatants can reach each other | Neutral combat — normal attacks and defence apply. |
| **Close-Quarters (CQ)** | You are **within the opponent's Natural Range** (typically the same or directly adjacent square) | Weapon leverage and manoeuvrability become core advantages/disadvantages. |

### How Close-Quarters Is Determined
If Distance ≤ Opponent's Natural Range → You are in Close-Quarters with them.

For humanoids this means **sharing a square or occupying an adjacent square within 1 square of them**.

This can occur even if **your weapon's reach is longer** — the space is too tight for full leverage.

### Effects of Close-Quarters
Close-Quarters modifies **weapon effectiveness**, not the ability to attack.

| Weapon Type / Trait | Effect in Close-Quarters |
|---|---|
| **Weapons with Close-Quarters** | Gain **Advantage** on melee Attack Tests in CQ. |
| **Weapons with Reach** | Suffer **Disadvantage** on melee Attack Tests **and** on Parry Tests while in CQ. |
| **Standard (neither Reach nor Close-Quarters)** | No inherent bonus or penalty. |

### Examples

| Scenario | Result |
|---|---|
| Dagger user closes to same square vs Spear | Dagger gains Advantage (if Close-Quarters), Spear suffers Disadvantage (Reach Dead Zone). |
| Sword vs Sword at 1 square | Neutral — neither Close-Quarters nor Reach impacts. |
| Axe user keeps distance at 2 squares vs Knife user | Knife user cannot attack; must close distance or reposition. |
