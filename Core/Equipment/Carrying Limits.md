Characters can only carry what their body can physically manage. Carrying capacity is determined by Might and represents the total load a character can bear before they are simply unable to move effectively.

## Carrying Capacity
**Capacity = 6 + (Might × 3)**

| Might | Capacity |
|---|---|
| 0 | 6 |
| 1 | 9 |
| 2 | 12 |
| 3 | 15 |
| 4 | 18 |
| 5 | 21 |
| 6 | 24 |

Characters cannot carry more than their capacity. There is no encumbrance penalty for approaching the limit — you either can carry it or you cannot.

## Item Weight Classes
Every item falls into one of four weight categories.

| Weight Class | Capacity Cost |
|---|---|
| Weightless | 0 |
| Light | 1 |
| Standard | 2 |
| Heavy | 3 |

**Weightless items** have no capacity cost individually. However every **20 of the same weightless item** counts as **1 Light item (1 capacity)**. Fractions do not count — 19 arrows cost nothing, 20 arrows cost 1.

> **Example:** A character carrying 45 standard arrows has 2 capacity units used (20 + 20), with 5 arrows left over at no cost.

## Worn Equipment
**Armour** does not contribute to carrying capacity. It is worn across the whole body and does not meaningfully add to load in the same way carried items do.

**Shields** contribute to carrying capacity normally using their weight class.

## Containers
Bags, packs, and similar containers increase carrying capacity by a flat amount defined individually. Their own weight class still applies toward capacity.

## Weapons and Shields
Weapons use their listed Weight Class for capacity cost. Shields use the following:

| Shield | Weight Class | Capacity Cost |
|---|---|---|
| Buckler | Light | 1 |
| Heater | Standard | 2 |
| Pavise | Heavy | 3 |
