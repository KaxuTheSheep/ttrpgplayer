# Roads & Infrastructure

*Maintained by a manufacturer consortium as baseline. Topped up by conglomerates in their own territory. Ignored everywhere else.*

---

## The Model

No government exists to maintain public infrastructure in New Halcyon. Roads are handled by a manufacturer consortium — an alliance of automotive and transport companies for whom functional roads are an existential business requirement. This provides baseline maintenance across the city: roads exist, are passable, and are occasionally repaired.

Beyond that baseline, individual conglomerates provide additional funding for road and infrastructure quality within their own territory. The incentive is twofold: operational (their own logistics require it) and reputational (quality infrastructure signals conglomerate strength and makes their districts more attractive to tenants and partners).

---

## What This Produces

Road quality is one of the most legible class and territory markers in New Halcyon. Within a few blocks of a conglomerate core: well-maintained surfaces, functioning lighting, drainage, clear signage. Moving outward: gradual deterioration. In forgotten districts with no conglomerate interest and no consortium attention: roads that have not been meaningfully repaired since the founding era, sometimes impassable, often dangerous.

The boundary between maintained and unmaintained road often marks the precise edge of conglomerate turf — sometimes literally from one side of a street to the other.
