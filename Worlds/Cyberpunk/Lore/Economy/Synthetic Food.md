# Synthetic Food

*The primary caloric consumption method in New Halcyon. Keeps you alive. Does not keep you well.*

---

## What It Is

Synthetic food is the Federal States' answer to losing its agricultural heartland to [[The United Territories]] in the split following [[The Catalyst (2039)]]. It is calorically complete — manufactured nutrition that provides sufficient energy intake to sustain a population. It is not nutritionally complete. Long-term exclusive consumption causes progressive micronutrient deficiency: weakened immunity, cognitive decline, bone deterioration, reduced life expectancy.

This is known. It is not hidden. It is simply the condition of living in New Halcyon if you cannot afford better.

---

## Production

Produced by conglomerate-owned manufacturing facilities within the Federal States. The technology exists, is scaled, and is profitable. It is cheap to produce and sold at a range of price points — the cheapest tiers are almost universally accessible, which is the point. A population that can afford calories but not nutrition is a population that is fed enough not to riot and sick enough not to organise.

Whether that outcome is designed or emergent is a question nobody in New Halcyon's power structure has any incentive to answer honestly.

---

## The Class Divide

Real food — specifically [[GMO Agricultural Imports|GMO agricultural imports]] from the United Territories (see [[GMO Agricultural Imports]]) — functions as a luxury micronutrient supplement in New Halcyon. Wealthy residents eat it regularly. Middle-tier residents eat it occasionally. Poor residents eat synthetic exclusively and show it over time — physically, cognitively, in ways that compound across generations.

The divide is visible. A person's diet can often be read from their appearance after enough years in the city.

---

## The Pill Problem

The Federal States is attempting to develop scalable micronutrient supplementation pills as a synthetic food add-on — see [[Micronutrient Research]]. These exist but are nowhere near population scale and are expensive. Currently a middle-class supplement rather than a public health solution.
