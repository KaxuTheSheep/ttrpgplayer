# Framework

*The base layer that makes augmentation possible. Not a product — a standard.*

---

## What a Framework Is

A framework is the foundational integration layer between a human body and its augmentations. It handles:

- Physical interfacing between biological tissue and hardware
- Signal routing between augments and the nervous system
- Identity and authentication for augment ecosystems
- Health monitoring and diagnostic functions
- Update and maintenance infrastructure

A framework is not an augment itself. It is the standard that determines what augments can connect, how they communicate, and what they can do. The analogy is a connection standard — the framework defines the port; augment manufacturers build to that port.

Having a framework does not make you augmented in any meaningful functional sense. It makes you *augmentable*. The capabilities come from whatever is built on top.

## The Two Framework Types

### SOMA (Standardised Open Modification Architecture)

The dominant framework standard. Used by over 90% of augmented individuals in the Federal States and adopted internationally including by [[Country C]].

SOMA is open-source. No single entity owns it. Any manufacturer can build augments to the SOMA standard without licensing fees or corporate permission.

This is both its strength and its commercial logic:

- The standard itself is free and widely available
- Augment manufacturers compete on implementation quality, not access
- The profit layer sits entirely in the augments, services, and subscriptions built on top of SOMA — not in the standard itself

SOMA is not a single product. It is a specification. Two SOMA-compatible augments from different manufacturers will integrate with each other to the standard — but performance, quality, and capability vary enormously. The analogy holds: all cables meeting the standard work, but there are levels to what they can actually do.

The SOMA standard was developed within the Federal States during the post-Catalyst fragmentation period and has since propagated internationally. It is maintained in a decentralised fashion — no governing body owns it, though various industry consortia publish documentation and manage version specifications.

#### SOMA in Country C

[[Country C]] adopted SOMA but uses it for purposes that diverge significantly from Federal States norms. Where FS augments are primarily about enhancing individual human capability, Country C's augment culture tends toward *body-as-platform* applications — using a consenting human body as a vessel for AI systems while the person's conscious attention is elsewhere. A citizen may allow an AI to operate their body for physical tasks while they are mentally occupied with something else entirely.

This is consensual and culturally normalised in Country C. It is a logical extension of their automation-driven society: if labour is the problem and human bodies are available, the efficient solution is voluntary human-AI integration. The aesthetic and application of augmentation looks radically different from the Federal States even though the underlying standard is the same.

### Proprietary Frameworks

An alternative class of frameworks that operate on entirely different standards to SOMA. Not SOMA-compatible. Not interoperable with the open ecosystem.

Proprietary frameworks are developed and maintained by specific organisations — primarily major conglomerates and state-adjacent entities. Every major conglomerate in New Halcyon competes in this space regardless of their primary sector, because proprietary frameworks are not primarily about augmentation markets.

They are about **control and opacity**.

A person operating on a proprietary framework is running on systems that outside parties cannot easily read, patch, interfere with, or surveil. For military applications, specialist operations, and organisations whose internal loyalty is sensitive — such as the [[Continental Freight Authority]] — proprietary frameworks provide a layer of operational security that SOMA, by its open nature, cannot.

Proprietary frameworks are expensive, restricted in availability, and require dedicated support infrastructure from their issuing organisation. They are not a consumer product.

## Augment Ownership Models

Not all augments work the same way commercially.

**Subscription augments** — the dominant corporate model. The augment hardware is cheap or subsidised; the capability is licensed on an ongoing basis. Missing a payment may result in reduced function, degraded performance, or complete deactivation depending on contract terms. Common for augments provided through employment or corporate benefit packages.

**Outright purchase** — less common but available, particularly for simpler augments and those sold by smaller manufacturers. Militias, independent operators, and cautious individuals tend to prefer this model. The augment is owned, not licensed.

**Grey market and homemade** — augments built to the SOMA standard outside corporate supply chains. Variable quality, no support contracts, no subscription requirements. Common in New Halcyon's forgotten districts. Legal status is ambiguous; enforcement is nonexistent in ungoverned areas.

## Physical Integration and Emigration

Frameworks physically integrate with the body over time. Removing augments is relatively straightforward. Changing frameworks is more difficult. Removing a framework entirely is a significant medical procedure.

The deeper problem for Federal States citizens considering emigration is not technical — it is biological. SOMA augments replace or supplement biological functions. Removing them leaves gaps that require biological material to fill: donor organs, grown tissue, biotech replacements. The [[The United Territories]] produces these components and controls access to them. Their willingness to provide them to departing Federal States citizens is limited and selective.
