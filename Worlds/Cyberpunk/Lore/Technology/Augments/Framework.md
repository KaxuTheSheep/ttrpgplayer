# Framework

*The base layer that makes augmentation possible. Not a product — a standard.*

---

## What a Framework Is

A framework is the foundational integration layer between a human body and its augmentations. It handles physical interfacing between biological tissue and hardware, signal routing between augments and the nervous system, identity and authentication for the augment ecosystem, health monitoring, and update and maintenance infrastructure.

A framework is not an augment itself. It is the standard that determines what augments can connect, how they communicate, and what they can do. Having a framework makes you augmentable. The capabilities come from what is built on top.

---

## SOMA — Standardised Open Modification Architecture

The dominant framework standard. Used by the vast majority of augmented individuals in the Federal States and adopted internationally.

SOMA is open source. No single entity owns it. Any manufacturer can build augments to the SOMA standard without licensing fees or corporate permission. The profit layer sits entirely in the augments, services, and subscriptions built on top — not in the standard itself.

SOMA was developed within the Federal States during the post-Catalyst fragmentation period and has since propagated internationally. It is maintained in a decentralised fashion — no governing body owns it, though industry consortia publish documentation and manage version specifications.

### SOMA in Country C

Country C adopted SOMA but applies it differently. Where Federal States augmentation is primarily about enhancing individual human capability, Country C's augment culture tends toward body-as-platform applications — a consenting citizen allowing an AI to operate their body for physical tasks while their conscious attention is elsewhere. Consensual and culturally normalised. The underlying standard is identical. The application looks radically different.

## Proprietary Frameworks

An alternative class of frameworks developed and maintained by specific organisations — primarily major conglomerates. Not SOMA compatible. Not interoperable with the open ecosystem.

Proprietary frameworks are not about augmentation markets. They are about control and opacity. A person running a proprietary framework is operating on systems that outside parties cannot easily read, patch, interfere with, or surveil. For military applications, specialist operations, and organisations whose internal loyalty is sensitive, this is the point.

Proprietary frameworks are expensive, restricted in availability, and require dedicated support infrastructure from their issuing organisation. They are not consumer products. They are issued to specific personnel for specific purposes.

The locked augment compatibility that comes with proprietary frameworks is a significant cost — the entire SOMA augment catalogue becomes inaccessible. This is acceptable only where the conglomerate provides everything the wearer needs through its own proprietary supply chain.

## Physical Integration and What It Means

Frameworks physically integrate with the body over time. Removing a framework is a significant medical procedure. More practically, augments installed on a framework replace or supplement biological functions — removing them leaves biological gaps that require material to fill.

This is the mechanism of the emigration trap. Federal States citizens considering leaving face not just the cost of de-augmentation but the biological reality of what remains after augments are removed. The United Territories produces the biological components needed to fill those gaps and controls access to them.

## The Unaugmented Framework

Some people have a framework installed with no augments connected to it. This is a common intermediate state — the framework is the entry cost to the augmentation ecosystem, and the augments follow as finances allow. Having a framework with nothing on it is functionally identical to having no augments. It is simply readiness.
