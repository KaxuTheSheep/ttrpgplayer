# Robotics

*Purpose built for one task. The form follows the function. Most look like nothing biological.*

---

## Design Principle

Robots in 2100 New Halcyon are not humanoid by default. A robot designed for one task looks like that task — optimised geometry, no unnecessary features, nothing present that does not serve the operational purpose. The visual result is varied and strange rather than uniformly anthropomorphic: a logistics robot looks nothing like a maintenance robot looks nothing like a security unit. What they share is efficiency of form.

The United Territories uses bioengineering rather than robotics for most applications where the FS would deploy automated systems — organisms rather than machines. The robots described here are primarily Federal States and Country C origin technology.

## Categories

### Logistics and Freight Robots

The most numerous and most visible robotic category in New Halcyon, given how much of the city's economy runs on supply chains that require constant physical movement of goods.

These do not resemble anything biological. Wheeled or tracked platforms optimised for load-bearing stability, articulated arms for manipulation and loading, sensor arrays for navigation in complex urban environments. Their design is purely functional — maximum cargo capacity, reliable navigation, minimal maintenance requirements.

The [[Continental Freight Authority]] operates logistics robots at scale in their port and freight operations. Clarent's distribution infrastructure depends on them for warehouse operation and last-kilometre delivery within their controlled territory.

**Class texture:** logistics robots in wealthy and conglomerate districts are current-generation, maintained, and carry corporate livery — the conglomerate's brand is on the machine moving their products. In forgotten districts older-generation logistics robots appear doing tasks they were not designed for, running degraded software, sometimes behaving unpredictably because their operational parameters no longer match their actual use. A logistics robot repurposed as a street vendor platform by a forgotten district resident is a specific kind of image.

### Maintenance and Construction Robots

Designed to access and operate in complex three-dimensional environments — building interiors, infrastructure crawlspaces, structural elements. Spider-like or multi-limbed geometry is common, with multiple tool-tipped appendages rather than hands. Small enough to access spaces humans cannot.

These work alongside the [[Self Healing Concrete & Materials|self-healing building materials]] and [[Building Management Systems|building management systems]] — the building's maintenance agent identifies a fault the self-healing material cannot address autonomously and dispatches a maintenance robot. In conglomerate buildings this process is invisible and continuous. In forgotten districts it does not happen.

Construction robots at the large scale handle tasks currently done by heavy machinery — with greater precision, continuous operation, and no requirement for the safety infrastructure human construction requires.

### Security Robots

The intersection of the robotics question and the faction structure. Security robots are force multipliers — not replacing human personnel but handling perimeter monitoring, access point enforcement, and low-level threat response so human judgment is reserved for situations that require it.

**Tier 1 conglomerate forces** deploy current-generation security robots with deliberately intimidating design — conglomerate branding, visual language that communicates consequence rather than neutrality. These are not trying to blend in. They are presence assertions.

**Tier 2 protection firms** use older-generation equipment at lower cost — functional but visibly lower tier than conglomerate hardware. The difference in hardware quality between a Tier 1 security robot and a Tier 2 one is readable at a glance to anyone who knows what they are looking at.

**Greyback Company** likely operates decommissioned military hardware — robots retired from Tier 1 conglomerate service that found their way into militia possession through salvage, purchase, or personnel who left corporate employment and took institutional knowledge with them. Greyback veterans know how to operate and maintain this hardware because they used the current-generation equivalents in their previous employment. Old but capable, operated by people who understand them.

### Agricultural and Industrial Robots

Less visible in New Halcyon specifically — the city is not agricultural and its industrial base is concentrated in conglomerate-owned facilities. Relevant primarily to the FS's synthetic food manufacturing and to the United Territories' factory farming infrastructure.

The UT's GMO agricultural facilities operate at scale and precision that human labour cannot maintain — but the UT achieves this through bioengineered organisms managing agricultural systems rather than conventional robotics. The distinction matters for the FS-UT relationship: an attack on UT agricultural infrastructure is not an attack on robots but on biological systems that are harder to replace on a timeline than hardware.

## Humanoid Exceptions

Humanoid robots exist in two specific use cases and almost nowhere else. Their humanoid form is not a default — it is an expensive, mechanically complex choice justified by specific operational requirements.

### Social Interface Roles

In contexts where humans respond differently to person-shaped interlocutors — reception, certain customer-facing roles, specific care contexts — humanoid form serves a functional purpose. The robot does not need to be humanoid to perform the task. The humans it interacts with respond better to something that reads as person-shaped.

These are often the least capable robots in terms of raw performance but among the most expensive to produce. The social performance requirement is demanding and the humanoid form factor is mechanically complex compared to purpose-optimised alternatives. They exist where the value of human comfort with the interaction justifies the cost.

### Infiltration and Surveillance

A robot that can move through human spaces without immediate identification as a robot has obvious intelligence and security applications. These exist as a capability held by conglomerates and state-adjacent actors — primarily Country B given their resources and their strategic interest in New Halcyon.

These are rare, expensive, and their existence is known about even if specific instances are not. The knowledge that humanoid infiltration robots exist at all produces a specific and diffuse paranoia in high-security contexts — verification protocols designed to identify non-humans, behavioural tells that trained personnel watch for, an arms race between detection capability and mimicry capability.

The social consequence is more interesting than any specific robot instance: in contexts where the stakes are high enough to justify deploying such hardware, no one can be fully certain that any given person is a person. That uncertainty, managed and distributed, is itself a tool.

## Relationship to AI

Robots in this world are not intelligent in any general sense. They run specialised agents — the same distributed, narrow-but-capable AI architecture that governs [[Building Management Systems|building management systems]] — optimised for their specific operational domain.

A logistics robot navigates and loads with extraordinary capability. It has no understanding of anything outside those functions. A security robot identifies threats and executes response protocols with high reliability. It does not make judgment calls.

The humanoid infiltration case is the exception — robots designed to pass as humans in social interaction require something closer to general conversational and behavioural capability, which is more expensive, more complex, and more difficult to maintain than purpose-built narrow systems. This is part of why they are rare.
