# Micronutrient Research

*The Federal States' attempt to solve the [[Synthetic Food]] deficiency problem. Not there yet.*

---

## The Programme

The Federal States — or more accurately, a combination of conglomerate-funded research bodies and what remains of Federal States institutional science — is developing a scalable micronutrient supplementation pill: a way to deliver the nutritional content absent from [[Synthetic Food]] in a cheap, mass-produceable format.

The technology exists in prototype. The science works at small scale. Population-scale production does not yet work — the cost per unit remains too high, the manufacturing process too complex, and the supply chains too fragile for the kind of citywide distribution that would make a meaningful public health difference.

---

## Current State

A middle-class supplement at best. Available, expensive, and bought by people who already have enough money to occasionally buy [[GMO Agricultural Imports]] anyway. Not reaching the populations most affected by synthetic-food deficiency.

The programme's stated goal is population-scale distribution. The realistic timeline for that goal is unclear, and there are structural incentives within the conglomerate ecosystem that do not favour a cheap solution to a problem that currently drives [[GMO Agricultural Imports|import demand]] and justifies [[The Reserve]]'s medical-adjacent financial products.
