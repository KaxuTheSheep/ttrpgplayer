# The Three Corporations

All three trace their roots to the founding coalition that built the origin world. All three are now independent, self-sustaining, and quietly competing to build a second one.

---
## Halvorsen Genetics
**Specialism:** Bioengineering, replicants ("Casts")

Founded by Halvorsen, who — by whatever means — is still alive and still in absolute personal control of the company centuries later. Halvorsen Genetics produces "Casts": engineered biological humans, grown to spec rather than born, used as labour and frontier personnel across all three corporations' territory (Halvorsen sells outward, not just to itself).

Casts come in a handful of common mass-produced models (3–6 major lines) plus hundreds of rarer, discontinued, or prototype types. Every Cast carries a unique 3D-depth QR-style marking beneath the left eye, encoding model, generation, and batch — effectively a permanent, unremovable serial number.

Officially, Casts are tools — obedient, replaceable, not people. Unofficially, certain lines (including the one your crew was grown from) are known to sometimes develop something indistinguishable from human self-awareness. Halvorsen Genetics does not acknowledge this.

## Kurosawa-Faulkner
**Specialism:** Androids, networked AI

Originally a British company, now under the dynastic control of the Kurosawa family — leadership passes by inheritance, not merit or committee. Kuro-Faulkner builds and operates androids: physical bodies that serve as outlets for a vast, hierarchical web of specialised AI systems, rather than housing any single mind.

No true general superintelligence exists — in this setting, none is thought to be necessary. Many smaller, specialised AI systems talking to each other in a chain of command do the job better. A small number of human overseers (roughly a few hundred per world, around ten per localised operational area) exist mostly as a legal and ethical failsafe rather than hands-on controllers.

Kuro-Faulkner synthetics have no survival instinct and no real stake in their own destruction — when a body is deemed unrecoverable, the AI system simply stops trying. This makes them efficient and expendable, but occasionally unpredictable in situations that require self-preserving judgement.

### Isolate-Line Synthetics
Not every Kuro-Faulkner synthetic operates as an outlet for the networked hierarchy. **Isolate-line** units are built for jobs where connection to the wider AI network is impractical, risky, or deliberately undesirable — deep frontier work, black-site security, anything where network disruption or intrusion could be catastrophic. Cut off from the hierarchy, they run on a more complex, self-sufficient core AI capable of semi-general judgement — not a true general intelligence, but considerably more independently competent than a standard networked unit.

This independence comes at a cost: Isolate-line cores are significantly harder and more expensive to produce than standard synthetics, which keeps them rare relative to networked units.

**Expansion Modules:** Isolate-line synthetics carry a core AI plus swappable expansion modules — discrete proficiency packages suited to the job at hand (combat, technical, medical, and so on). Modules can be swapped out between assignments, letting a single unit be re-tasked without a full rebuild.

Officially, Isolate-line units are simply a specialised product line. Whether their semi-general judgement occasionally edges closer to something the "no true AGI is necessary" doctrine wouldn't like to admit is not a question Kuro-Faulkner leadership enjoys being asked.

## Ossuary Dynamics
**Specialism:** Augmentation, brain-casing, network infrastructure

Founded by an eccentric, gothic-obsessed individual whose personal aesthetic — cathedral architecture fused with heavy machinery — still defines much of the company's older infrastructure. Augmentation began as a cosmetic fashion product. After the founder's death, a stockholder council took over and shifted priority from fashion to function; fashion-oriented augments still exist but are a sideline now. There is ongoing internal tension between old-guard traditionalists loyal to the gothic aesthetic and a newer faction pushing to modernise the company's image entirely.

Ossuary's signature technology is full-conversion brain-casing: a person's organic brain is removed and encased, monitored via neural interface, and used to operate a fully synthetic body — dramatically reducing resource needs (no food, minimal water, mostly electricity) while retaining a real human mind, complete with survival instinct and emotion.

This is elective technology for the corporation's own people. It is also, separately, what your crew chose for themselves after escaping — not for efficiency, but to physically erase every trace of what you used to be.

At the highest tier, Ossuary also researches consciousness backup — an imperfect, experimental technology that keeps a desynchronised backup of a person's mind. Dying reverts you to your last backup: you survive, but lose everything since. Whether the copy is really "you" is not something anyone can answer with confidence.
