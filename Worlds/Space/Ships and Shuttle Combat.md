# Ships and Shuttle Combat

## The Mothership
Your mothership functions like a satellite station bolted to a set of engines — closer to Interstellar's *Endurance* than a traditional starship. It rarely enters a planet's gravity well or true deep-space combat directly. It carries your cryopods, fuel reserves, fabrication systems, and cargo. Losing it is catastrophic. Destroying an enemy's mothership outright is usually counterproductive — everyone would rather disable, board, and take it intact.

## Shuttles
Combat and fieldwork happen in shuttles: small, fast, dual-use craft (both high-orbit and atmospheric capable) crewed by 2–3 people. Each shuttle carries months of fuel — plenty for extended operations, but not infinite. Running a shuttle dry to chase a fight or extend a mission is a real, sometimes necessary, sometimes fatal choice.

### Shuttle Roles
- **Pilot** — traversal, positioning, dodging incoming fire.
- **Gunner** — offense, accuracy, focus-fire decisions.
- **Shipwright** — manages repair and [[Damage Types|damage]] mitigation in real time during combat.

## Combat
Shuttle combat is close, personal, and attritional — think small-boat skirmishes, not capital ship duels.

Every shuttle has two material pools:
- **Raw material** — a reserve that doesn't directly stop [[Damage Types|damage]].
- **Ready-to-use material** — converted from raw material over time, and this is what actually offsets incoming [[Damage Types|damage]].

A shipwright's real job is keeping conversion ahead of incoming [[Damage Types|damage]]. Sustained, accurate fire that outpaces conversion is how a shuttle actually goes down — not a single big hit. Concentrated fire on the same spot beats a ship's passive regeneration (if it has any); spread fire tests total capacity instead.

Different hull materials behave differently — some self-heal passively unless struck repeatedly in the same place, some convert raw-to-ready faster but never regenerate on their own, some have a higher ceiling but cost more to refill. Your ship starts with a standard Ossuary-built hull, but is fully open to modification — new weapons, new materials, new geometry — as you acquire the means.

## Losing a Fight
A defeated shuttle is either destroyed outright or disabled ("sunk"). Crew cannot remain aboard a critically damaged shuttle for long — escape pods, small craft with an automated return-to-mothership system, are the way out. Hesitating too long to eject is usually the actual cause of death, not the enemy's last shot.

## Boarding
Because destroying an enemy's mothership is rarely the goal, most serious engagements end in boarding: disable their shuttles, get aboard their mothership, remove or subdue whoever's still there, and take what you came for — cargo, technology, or the ship itself.
