# People and Bodies

By 2300, humanity's population is well past 100 billion. A "person" in this setting can mean several different things physically, even if the mind inside is (probably) still human.

## Baseline Humans
Fully organic. Numerous, but not as expendable as Casts or synthetics — still the most resource-costly to raise and train. Retain full survival instinct and the full range of human emotion, for better and worse.

## Casts (Halvorsen Genetics)
Engineered biological humans, grown rather than born, produced in a handful of common models plus many rarer variants. Casts have genuine survival instinct — enough biology intact for real fight-or-flight — which makes them more self-sufficient than androids but also more unpredictable than their manufacturer would like. Officially "tools." Unofficially, a live and unresolved question.

Identifiable by a 3D-depth marking beneath the left eye, encoding their model and batch.

## Synthetics (Kurosawa-Faulkner)
Fully artificial bodies operated as physical outlets for a networked AI hierarchy — not individual minds. No survival instinct, no fear of "death" in any real sense. Extremely repairable and replaceable, but require human oversight for judgement calls the AI system won't reliably make on its own.

## Augmented / Brain-Cased (Ossuary Dynamics)
A human brain, removed from its original body and encased, operating a synthetic body via neural-net translation. Retains full human emotion and survival instinct, while gaining synthetic-grade physical repairability. The most reliable frontier operators of the three types — and the most emotionally exposed.

Augmentation exists on a spectrum: minor cybernetic enhancement and network access is common and mostly cosmetic/practical (a matter of taste and role, not wealth); full-body conversion is a much bigger, more deliberate step, associated with combat and frontier roles.

## Your Crew
You are fully converted — brain-cased, synthetic-bodied, network-capable — having chosen full conversion after escaping your original lives as Casts. It wasn't just about the practical advantages. It was about making sure nothing about your old body could ever be used to identify or reclaim you again.

## Networks and Hacking
Augmented crew, synthetics, and androids can link into local networks — useful for coordination in the field, and a real vulnerability in a fight. Hostile network intrusion is common and dangerous, but full hostile body-puppeting is rare and difficult; most network [[Attacks|attacks]] manifest as disruption, jamming, or momentary loss of control rather than complete takeover. Anti-intrusion hardware is standard issue on any properly maintained network card.
