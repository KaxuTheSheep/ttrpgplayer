# Travel

## Wormholes
Traversable wormholes require exotic matter (negative-mass matter) to hold the throat open. They only connect two specific, already-linked, already-discovered locations — you cannot wormhole somewhere new. They are not permanently open: activation consumes exotic matter for every moment they're switched on, so routes run on schedules and operational windows rather than being freely available on demand. Missing your window has consequences.

## The Alcubierre Drive
A warp-bubble drive, using exotic matter to fold space around a ship rather than travelling through it conventionally. Unlike wormholes, an Alcubierre-capable ship can travel point-to-point to anywhere — including completely unmapped space — at the cost of consuming exotic matter proportional to distance travelled. Enormously expensive. Very few ships are fitted with one; those that are tend to be high-priority corporate, military, or emergency vessels. Your ship almost certainly isn't one of them.

## Standard Interstellar Travel
Most ships, including yours, rely on fusion torch and antimatter drives at sub-light speeds. Reaching an unmapped system means a long haul — weeks to months of travel — with the crew in cryosleep for the duration. You'll watch your point of origin shrink and, eventually, a new system grow, and feel every bit like a small moving part in something much larger.

## In-System Travel
Within a system, ships use the cheaper, more common fusion torch drive exclusively — antimatter is reserved for interstellar hauls. Your mothership rarely descends into a gravity well; instead, smaller shuttle craft handle both high-orbit operations and atmospheric work.
