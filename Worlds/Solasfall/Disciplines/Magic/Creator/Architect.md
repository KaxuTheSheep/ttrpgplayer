# Architect
![[Architect.png]]
## Theoretical Foundation
**Prerequisites:** None.  
**Cost:** 2 Trait Points.
You understand the underlying principles of spell construction well enough to teach others. When assisting another character in spell construction during downtime, you can contribute your Insight bonus to their Tests as a collaborative bonus (+2 per assisting Architect, to a maximum of +6). You also reduce the time required for any spell research you personally undertake by **1 downtime period** (GM's discretion on the baseline duration).

## Efficient Research
**Prerequisites:** Theoretical Foundation.  
**Cost:** 2 Trait Points.
When developing a new spell during downtime, you require only **half** the usual material costs for the development process. You've learned to substitute expensive or rare components with more common alternatives without sacrificing quality.

## Conceptual Leap
**Prerequisites:** Theoretical Foundation.  
**Cost:** 3 Trait Points.
Once per spell you develop, you may declare a **Conceptual Leap** — skipping one full stage of the development process without needing to roll. This does not grant automatic success; it simply removes that stage from the required progression. The GM determines which stage is skipped based on the spell's complexity and your explanation of how your understanding bridges the gap.

## Variable Understanding
**Prerequisites:** None.  
**Cost:** 2 Trait Points.
When modifying an existing spell during downtime (morphing a spell), reduce the development time and resource expenditure by **half** (rounded down). You understand the spell's internal logic well enough to make surgical changes without rebuilding from scratch.

## Flexible Framework
**Prerequisites:** Variable Understanding.  
**Cost:** 3 Trait Points.
You may prepare a spell during downtime in **two different configurations** simultaneously (different variable distributions). This is a single prepared "spell slot" that you can choose between when casting. For Weave Casting, this is a single circle with two activation modes.

## Structural Insight
**Prerequisites:** None.  
**Cost:** 2 Trait Points.
When encountering a pre-existing spell framework (whether in a scroll, an institution's curriculum, or a captured enemy's circle), you can identify its core variables and rough Manifest/Control DNs with a successful Insight Test (DN set by GM). This does not teach you the spell, but tells you what it does and how difficult it is to learn.

## Reverse Engineering
**Prerequisites:** Structural Insight.  
**Cost:** 3 Trait Points.
When you personally witness a spell being cast (or examine a fully intact Weave circle), you may spend downtime attempting to reverse-engineer it. This uses the standard spell construction rules but requires no physical material costs (you're replicating, not inventing) and has its time reduced by **half**. The GM sets the Complexity DN based on how much of the process you observed.

## Spell Theory
**Prerequisites:** None.  
**Cost:** 2 Trait Points.
You understand magical theory well enough to predict how spells interact. When you witness an ongoing magical effect, you may spend an Action to attempt an Insight Test (DN set by GM) to determine:
- The spell's School
- Approximately how many Enhancement Points were invested
- If the spell is Flux or Weave (and if Weave, whether Woven or Drawn)
- Any visible Defects or Backlash potential

## Theoretical Countermeasure
**Prerequisites:** Spell Theory.  
**Cost:** 3 Trait Points.
When you successfully identify a spell using Spell Theory, you gain a **+2 bonus** to any Defensive Actions taken against that specific casting (Dodge, Block, etc.) — you understand enough about its structure to anticipate its behaviour.

## Mana Economizer
**Prerequisites:** None.  
**Cost:** 2 Trait Points.
You've learned to minimise waste in your spells' mana flow. The first Enhancement Point you invest in **any** spell (Flux or Weave) per day does not increase either Control DN or Manifest DN. This resets after a Long Rest.

## Esoteric Understanding
**Prerequisites:** Efficient Research, Flexible Framework, Mana Economizer.  
**Cost:** 4 Trait Points.
You may attempt to develop spells that push the boundaries of what is conventionally understood. With GM permission, you can research spells that:
- Combine two School effects (e.g., Lightning + Frost for cryo-electric)
- Have unusual trigger conditions
- Produce effects considered "difficult" or "rare" in your setting

These spells take longer to develop (GM sets increased time) and require more resources, but are uniquely yours.

## Living Theory
**Prerequisites:** Esoteric Understanding.  
**Cost:** 4 Trait Points.
Once per session, when a spell you cast would normally fail (Flux below Control DN or Weave below Defect DN), you may choose to succeed instead — but the spell manifests in an unexpected way. The GM determines the exact outcome, but it is always:
- Thematically consistent
- Not actively harmful to the caster
- Possibly beneficial, possibly inconvenient, always strange

You've learned so much about spell theory that even your failures reveal new possibilities.
