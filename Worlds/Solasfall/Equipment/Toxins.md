Toxins are substances that introduce harmful effects into a target's body. They are divided into two categories based on their origin and typical delivery method.

**Venoms** are biological in origin, typically harvested from creatures. They are most effective when injected directly into the bloodstream.
**Poisons** are refined or cultivated substances. They are most effective when ingested or inhaled.

These categories are not rigid — any toxin can be delivered through any valid method. However each toxin has a **preferred application** that reflects how it works most effectively.

## Application Methods
| Method | Description |
|---|---|
| Injected | Delivered via piercing weapon, bite, or similar. Requires breaking the skin. |
| Ingested | Delivered via food, drink, or forced consumption. |
| Inhaled | Delivered via powder, gas, or similar airborne means. |

**Preferred Application:** When a toxin is delivered via its preferred method, the effect DN increases by +3, making it harder to resist.

## Coating Weapons
Toxins can be applied onto a weapon at the cost of a half action. A toxin vial contains enough substance for **10 applications**. Each hit against a target consumes one application (10% of the vial). Unused applications on a weapon degrade after combat. 

## Antidotes
Every toxin has a specific antidote. Antidotes are not interchangeable — the antidote for Thornbleed will not treat Hollowdust. Antidotes are consumed on use and immediately end all active effects of their associated toxin.

# Venoms

### Thornbleed
A venom harvested from the barbed spines of a Hollowdeep creature. Causes rapid internal bleeding that bypasses the body's natural resilience.

**Preferred Application:** Injected
**Resist:** Vitality Test DN 13 on application. On success, no effect.
**Effect:** On fail, the target takes **1d4 Blood damage per round**, bypassing Sturdiness.
**Duration:** 4 rounds, then ends automatically.
**Antidote:** Thornbleed Antidote

---

# Poisons

### Gravewort Extract
A bitter extract derived from the Gravewort plant. Thins the blood and prevents wounds from healing naturally.

**Preferred Application:** Ingested
**Resist:** Vitality Test DN 13 on application. On success, no effect.
**Effect:** On fail:
- Wounds cannot be removed on any rest.
- Disadvantage on all Medicine Tests to close wounds.
**Duration:** Until antidote or 1 hour out of combat.
**Antidote:** Gravewort Antidote

---

### Stoneveil
A refined toxin that seeps into muscle tissue and dulls physical capability. Affects both strength and coordination simultaneously.

**Preferred Application:** Injected
**Resist:** Vitality Test DN 13 on application and each round thereafter. Two consecutive successful resists end the effect.
**Effect:** On fail, the target suffers **–3 to all Might and Grace rolls**.
**Duration:** Until two consecutive resists or antidote.
**Antidote:** Stoneveil Antidote

---

### Ashdelay
A fine powder that enters the lungs and lies dormant before triggering a catastrophic internal reaction. The target may feel nothing until it is too late.

**Preferred Application:** Inhaled
**Resist:** Vitality Test DN 13 each round for 3 rounds. Succeeding any resist roll shakes off the poison before it triggers.
**Effect:** If the target fails all 3 resist rolls, they take **3d6 Blood damage** bypassing Sturdiness at the end of round 3.
**Duration:** 3 rounds, then resolves.
**Antidote:** Ashdelay Antidote

---

### Mireveil
A hallucinogenic compound that distorts perception and fractures the mind. Victims see things that are not there and struggle to process reality.

**Preferred Application:** Ingested
**Resist:** Vitality Test DN 13 each round. Success ends the effect.
**Effect:** While active:
- **–2 to all Insight and Perception Tests.**
- **1d6 Sanity damage per round.**
**Duration:** Until a successful resist or antidote.
**Antidote:** Mireveil Antidote

---

### Hollowdust
A paralytic powder derived from deep Hollowdeep fauna. Shuts down voluntary muscle control rapidly and completely.

**Preferred Application:** Inhaled
**Resist:** Vitality Test DN 16 on application. Each round thereafter, Vitality Test DN 16 to end the effect.
**Effect:** On fail, the target gains the [[Conditions#Paralysed|Paralysed]] condition.
**Duration:** Until a successful resist or antidote.
**Antidote:** Hollowdust Antidote

---

### Dreamroot
A sedative compound distilled from a bioluminescent Hollowdeep fungus. Induces progressive unconsciousness by stacking exhaustion rapidly.

**Preferred Application:** Ingested
**Resist:** Vitality Test DN 13 on application only. On success, no effect.
**Effect:** On fail, the target gains **1 level of Exhaustion per round**, up to a maximum of **3 additional Exhaustion levels** from this poison.
**Duration:** 3 rounds, then ends automatically.
**Antidote:** Dreamroot Antidote

---

### Veilbane
A refined toxin that disrupts the soul's conducting ability, interfering with mana flow and destabilising spellcasting entirely.

**Preferred Application:** Injected
**Resist:** Vitality Test DN 13 each round. Success ends the effect.
**Effect:** While active, the target has **Disadvantage on all casting Tests**.
**Duration:** Until a successful resist or antidote.
**Antidote:** Veilbane Antidote
