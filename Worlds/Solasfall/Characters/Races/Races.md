The city of **[[Solasfall]]** is home to approximately **275,000 inhabitants**, making it a bustling metropolis by pre-industrial standards. It is large enough to sustain a complex society yet still vulnerable to the hardships of a world without industrial technology.

Within this population, diverse **races** contribute to the city’s culture, economy, and politics. Each race brings unique customs, abilities, and social challenges, resulting in a complex web of alliances, tensions, and coexistence. Interracial friendships and partnerships exist, but systemic inequality remains deeply embedded, influencing every district, guild, and institution.
### Races in [[Solasfall]]
Races in [[Solasfall]] include:
- **[[Human]]** – The most common race, [[Human|humans]] are versatile and resilient. They dominate the city's politics, guilds, and military, often acting as mediators between other races. Known for adaptability, ambition, and ingenuity.
- **[[Stitched]]** – Constructed beings [[stitched]] together from multiple past lives. Feared for their association with war and necromantic taboo, they are tolerated for their resilience and tireless endurance. Many serve as labourers, explorers, or mercenaries.
- **[[Velkhari]]** – Transformed by a [[Hollowdeep]] parasite that overwrites their former self entirely, [[Velkhari]] are predatory humanoids marked by pale skin, shark-like teeth, and pupil-less eyes. Feared and widely mistrusted, they are tolerated for their martial usefulness and connection to [[Rift]] energy. Their sparring culture and individual meritocracy set them apart from every other race in the city.
- **[[Kenku]]** – Avian humanoids integrated throughout [[Solasfall]]'s social fabric. Adaptable and quietly influential, they fill most societal niches and are respected for their loyalty and mimicry. Drawn toward shadow and memory, many follow [[The Blind Shepherd]].
- **[[Murk]]** – Amphibious humanoids with grey-green skin and long limbs, found throughout [[Solasfall]]'s Market and Entertainment Districts. Valued for their physical capability and craftsmanship, they are tolerated rather than feared — practical, patient, and quietly enduring.
- **[[Cindral]]** – Fire-touched humanoids with obsidian skin that cracks with inner flame and eyes that burn with steady light. Scorned as betrayers of [[The Emberwright]] and now devoted to [[The Pale Bloom]], they are spiritually conflicted but fiercely resilient artisans and warriors.
- **[[Drenek]]** – Broad, earthen humanoids with stone-like skin and a natural command of their own weight. Relegated to labour roles in mines and sewers despite their [[Skills|endurance]] and craftsmanship, they are the city's backbone — oath-bound, stoic, and deeply undervalued.
