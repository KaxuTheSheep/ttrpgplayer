# Status
*Alien but accepted.*
Amphibious and tall, with grey-green skin and long limbs, Murks are a quiet but present part of the city. Often found in the Market and Entertainment Districts, where their flexibility and [[Speed & Resolution|speed]] are valued. Generally scattered throughout [[Solasfall]] but holding lower social status — slightly beneath Kenku in societal standing. Though alien in appearance, Murks are tolerated rather than feared or pitied. Valued for physical capability and craftsmanship, though often underestimated or overlooked.
# Culture
Murks favour learning practical, physical [[Skills|skills]] — [[Skills|crafting]], combat, and [[Skills|endurance]]-based trades. Their culture is shaped by a sense of impermanence; they accept their shorter lifespans and seek to live fully. Social bonds resemble loose communal groupings, more fluid than rigid family units, akin to amphibious spawning pools or seasonal gatherings.

The Emberwright Faith is common among Murks, resonating with their view of life as a fleeting forge of purpose. They maintain a quiet, patient outlook toward hardship and change, preferring [[Actions|action]] over debate. Within their districts, Murks thrive as artisans, warriors, and traders — roles they may be denied elsewhere. Their neighbourhoods preserve old traditions from their surface origins, maintaining ancestral rites, symbols, and seasonal ceremonies.
# Relationships with Other Races
Humans are viewed as weak-bodied but admired for their perseverance and adaptability. Stitched are fascinating to Murks — they are intrigued by the paradox of deathless decay. Kenku are disliked, found too opportunistic or performative. Velkhari are seen as strong but lazy, obsessed with fighting rather than purpose. Drenek are met with curiosity but uncertainty. Cindral are distrusted deeply — Murks believe they betrayed something sacred in the fire or forge.
# Appearance
Murks are amphibious, with smooth, moist skin ranging from green-grey to slate-blue hues. Their eyes are large and reflective, adapted to dim light, and their limbs are long and flexible. Clothing tends toward fitted wraps and waterproof fabrics, designed for [[Movement Types|movement]] both on land and underwater. Many decorate their skin with bioluminescent inks or carved symbols that glow faintly in the dark.

# Traits

## Amphibious Breathing
You can breathe underwater indefinitely and on land.
## Water Adaptation
You have Advantage on [[Skills|Athletics]] Tests related to swimming and climbing slippery or wet surfaces.
## Low Light Vision
Your Clear Zone extends into what would normally be your Blurry Zone up to 9m. You cannot see in total darkness.

## Tongue Lash
Prerequisites: None.
Cost: 2 Trait Points. 
As an [[Attacks|attack]], you may lash out with your tongue at a target within 4.5m, dealing 1d4 + [[Attributes|Grace]] Bludgeoning [[Damage Types|damage]] at [[Speed & Resolution|Speed]] 12 + [[Attributes|Grace]]. You may also use your tongue to grab or retrieve small unattended objects within [[Range & Threat|range]] as a Half [[Actions|Action]], though not to manipulate tools or weapons.

## Improved Tongue Lash
Prerequisites: Tongue Lash.
Cost: 1 Trait Point. 
Your tongue strike sharpens. [[Damage Types|Damage]] increases to 1d6 + [[Attributes|Grace]]. [[Range & Threat|Range]] increases to 6m.

## Enhanced Tongue Lash
Prerequisites: Improved Tongue Lash. 
Cost: 1 Trait Point. 
[[Damage Types|Damage]] increases to 1d8 + [[Attributes|Grace]]. [[Range & Threat|Range]] increases to 7.5m. Your tongue can now manipulate tools and weapons within [[Range & Threat|range]] and may attempt to disarm held weapons from enemies as a Half [[Actions|Action]] contested by the target's [[Attributes|Might]] or [[Attributes|Grace]] Test.

## Tongue Grapple
Prerequisites: Tongue Lash. 
Cost: 3 Trait Points. 
You may use your tongue to grapple a target within Tongue Lash [[Range & Threat|range]] instead of dealing [[Damage Types|damage]]. The grapple functions as a standard grapple but originates from your tongue rather than your body — displacement and positioning are based on the tongue's reach rather than your own. While maintaining the tongue grapple you retain full use of your body, including attacking with weapons and moving freely.

## Swift Swimmer
Prerequisites: None. 
Cost: 2 Trait Points. 
Whenever you make an [[Attacks|attack]] while underwater, you may move a number of metres equal to half your Walk Travel Rate per [[Speed & Resolution|Speed]] point as part of that [[Attacks|attack]].

## Mastered Swimmer
Prerequisites: Swift Swimmer. 
Cost: 2 Trait Points. 
Your underwater combat mobility improves. When making an [[Attacks|attack]] underwater, you may move a number of metres equal to your full Walk Travel Rate per [[Speed & Resolution|Speed]] point as part of that [[Attacks|attack]].

## Leap Strike
Prerequisites: None. 
Cost: 2 Trait Points. 
When you make a melee [[Attacks|attack]] immediately after a Jump, you may choose one of the following on hit:
- Deal +3 [[Damage Types|damage]].
- Knock the target Prone ([[Attributes|Might]] Test DN 12 to resist).

## Improved Leap Strike
Prerequisites: Leap Strike. 
Cost: 1 Trait Point. 
The [[Damage Types|damage]] bonus increases to +5. The Prone resistance DN increases to 14.

## Enhanced Leap Strike
Prerequisites: Improved Leap Strike.
Cost: 1 Trait Point. 
The [[Damage Types|damage]] bonus increases to +7. The Prone resistance DN increases to 16.

## Toxin
Prerequisites: Tongue Lash, Exclusive to Camouflage. 
Cost: 3 Trait Points. 
Your toxin glands mature, naturally producing Thornbleed venom. Your Tongue Lash [[Attacks|attack]] now delivers Thornbleed on hit in addition to its normal [[Damage Types|damage]]. The target must make a [[Attributes|Vitality]] Test DN 13 or suffer the effects of [[Toxins#Thornbleed|Thornbleed]]. This is a biological output — no vial or item is consumed. The venom replenishes naturally and has no usage limit.

## Camouflage
Prerequisites: Exclusive to Toxin. 
Cost: 2 Trait Points. 
You can consciously shift your skin pigmentation to match your surroundings. Activating or deactivating camouflage is a free [[Actions|action]] requiring deliberate focus. While camouflaged and remaining completely still, you have Advantage on [[Skills|Stealth]] Tests. Camouflage breaks immediately upon moving or attacking and must be reactivated. Only your skin camouflages — clothing and armour are unaffected, limiting this ability to lightly armoured Murks.
