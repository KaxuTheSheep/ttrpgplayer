Magic is the practice of harnessing [[Mana]] — quantum particles that flow through all living things, the land, and the air. Mana manipulates whatever it contacts by increasing or decreasing its properties. Lifebinders increase cellular activity to accelerate healing. Fire mages increase the oxidation rate and thermal energy of existing fuel to produce combustion. The magic does not conjure from nothing — it works with what is already present, forcing properties to shift beyond what they would do unaided.

Mana cannot create something from nothing. It can only manipulate what exists. The scope of what a mage can affect is limited only by their understanding of the process they are trying to influence and their ability to control the mana they direct.

Only trained individuals or divine beings can harness mana effectively. Untrained exposure to mana flow produces random, unpredictable effects.

## The Soul as Conductor
Mages channel mana by using their soul as a conductor — a sorting mechanism through which mana is drawn from the ambient environment, shaped, and directed toward an intended effect. The soul does not store mana. It conducts it. Mana passes through the caster during casting and dissipates after.

This means a mage can theoretically cast indefinitely. There is no mana pool to deplete. The limiting factor is the soul's capacity to handle the flow. Forcing too much mana through too quickly causes [[Mana Overload Syndrome]] — a catastrophic backlash that can permanently sever the soul's conducting ability, leaving the mage unable to feel or direct mana at all.

# The Three Casting Methods
Magic is expressed through three distinct casting methods. All use the same underlying mana system but differ in when structure is applied and how control is enforced.

## Flux Casting (Live Casting)
Flux Casting is the real-time shaping of mana as it passes through the caster. It is immediate, adaptive, and highly flexible, but inherently unstable due to the lack of a pre-defined structure.

### Core Properties
- Fastest decision-to-effect casting method
- Highest flexibility and improvisation
- Highest variance in outcome
- No preparation required
- Most sensitive to control errors

### Mechanical Identity
Flux Casting uses:
- Control DN (stability threshold)
- Manifest DN (full resolution threshold)
- Attribute-based shaping (Insight, Resolve, Presence)
- Enhancement scaling (increases power and instability)

### Key Principle
Flux Casting requires the caster to form, stabilise, and release mana in real time. Small control deviations can cause major outcome shifts.

## Weave Casting (Structured Magic)
Weave Casting uses a constructed magical framework — circle, rune, or geometric sigil — that constrains mana into a predefined pattern. Unlike Flux Casting, the spell's structure exists before or during activation, not during freeform shaping. Weave Casting trades flexibility for consistency and control.

### Core Properties
- Medium casting speed (varies depending on construction)
- Moderate stability
- Requires physical or inscribed structure
- Can be interrupted during construction (if mid-combat)
- More consistent than Flux Casting, less flexible than it

### Subtype: Woven Casting
Woven Casting is a fully prepared magical structure that exists before activation. It represents the most stable form of structured magic. The structure is prepared beforehand and mana is introduced to activate it.

**Core Properties:**
- Fastest activation of structured magic
- Highest consistency and reliability
- Requires preparation time outside of combat
- Minimal real-time control required
- Output is highly deterministic

### Subtype: Drawn Weave Casting
Drawn Weave Casting is a mid-combat construction of a magical structure. The caster actively inscribes or forms the structure while under pressure.

**Core Properties:**
- Slower than Flux Casting, faster than full preparation methods
- Requires uninterrupted or semi-stable casting conditions
- Moderate flexibility during construction
- Less consistent than Woven Casting
- Structure must be completed before full effect resolves

### Key Principle
Weave Casting constrains mana through structure. Woven Casting executes a prepared structure. Drawn Weave Casting constructs structure under pressure.

# Casting Comparison

| Method              | Speed              | Stability | Flexibility | Preparation             |
| ------------------- | ------------------ | --------- | ----------- | ----------------------- |
| Flux Casting        | Fastest execution  | Low       | Very high   | None                    |
| Drawn Weave Casting | Medium             | Medium    | Medium      | Partial (in-combat)     |
| Woven Casting       | Fast activation    | High      | Low         | Full pre-combat setup   |

# Radiant and Umbral Magic
Radiant and Umbral damage types cannot be produced by trained mages regardless of skill. These forces originate from deities — mana constructs sustained by collective belief — and can only be channelled through Followers. No amount of Insight, Resolve, or Presence grants a mage access to these types.
