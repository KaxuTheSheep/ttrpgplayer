Flux Casting is the real-time shaping of [[mana]] as it flows through the caster’s soul. It is the most direct form of magic — no structure, no pre-defined containment, only controlled acceleration of natural processes as they are being formed.
Because the spell is built at the moment of release, Flux Casting is highly flexible but inherently unstable. Even minor deviations in control can cascade into dramatically different outcomes.
## Core Principle

Flux Casting operates under the fundamental nature of magic:

> Magic is chaotic in the mathematical sense. Small variations in control produce wildly different outputs.

A skilled caster reduces this chaos through precision. A novice allows it to express itself freely.

A Flux spell never simply “fails.” It always resolves into something real — even if unintended.

## Casting a Flux Spell
Every spell has a **Base DN** split into two thresholds:
- **Control DN** — minimum stability required to keep the spell coherent
- **Manifest DN** — threshold required to fully realise the intended effect
To cast a Flux spell, roll:
> **2d10 + chosen Mental [[Attributes|Attribute]]**

Then compare the result to both DN values.

|Result|Outcome|
|---|---|
|Meets or exceeds Manifest DN|Full Success — spell resolves as intended|
|Meets Control DN but not Manifest DN|Partial Success — spell manifests, but distorted, reduced, or unpredictable|
|Below Control DN|Backlash — spell misfires into an uncontrolled but real magical effect|

## Mental Attribute Selection
When casting, you choose one Mental [[Attributes|Attribute]]. Only that Attribute applies to the cast.

| [[Attributes\|Attribute]] | Effect                                                                                                                                               |
| --------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| [[Attributes\|Insight]]   | Determines how many enhancement points you control precisely. Points beyond your [[Attributes\|Insight]] score randomise.                                            |
| [[Attributes\|Resolve]]   | Reduces both DNs after points are applied. Every 1 [[Attributes\|Resolve]] reduces Control DN by 1 and Manifest DN by 1.                                             |
| [[Attributes\|Presence]]  | Amplifies specific spell effects after resolution at no additional cost. Each spell defines which of its effects [[Attributes\|Presence]] amplifies and by how much. |

## Enhancement Points
Spells can be modified by investing Enhancement Points into variables such as range, damage, area, or special effects.
- Each point increases:
    - **Control DN by +1**
    - **Manifest DN by +2**
- Enhancement beyond your [[Attributes|Insight]] introduces instability — those points become unpredictable in effect.
- There is no upper limit to enhancement investment, only increasing risk and difficulty.

Flux Casting rewards ambition but punishes overreach.

## [[Sanity]] Cost
All Flux Casting strains the caster’s mind and soul.

|Outcome|Cost|
|---|---|
|Full Success|Base cost + 1 per enhancement point spent|
|Partial Success|Half total cost|
|Backlash|Double total cost|

Each spell defines its own base [[Sanity]] cost.

## Backlash
If the cast fails to meet Control DN, [[mana]] does not dissipate harmlessly.
Instead, it is expelled outward in an uncontrolled catalytic reaction.
Backlash effects are always:
- Thematically related to the spell
- Physically real
- Unpredictable in form or direction

Examples include:
- Overexpansion of effect radius
- Inverted elemental properties
- Misfiring onto unintended targets
- Delayed or unstable detonation
- Environmental distortion around the caster

Flux Casting guarantees consequence — even failure has presence.
