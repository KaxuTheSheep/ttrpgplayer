Weave Casting is the structured formation of [[mana]] into a predefined geometric or symbolic framework. Unlike Flux Casting, where [[mana]] is shaped in real time, Weave Casting constrains [[mana]] through external structure before or during its flow.

These structures take the form of **circles, sigils, and rune networks**, which encode instructions that [[mana]] follows when activated.

Weave Casting trades flexibility for stability: the caster does not shape reality moment-to-moment, but instead builds a system that defines how reality should behave when [[mana]] is applied.

## Core Principle
Weave Casting functions on the principle that:
> [[mana]] behaves predictably when constrained by sufficiently precise structure.

Instead of controlling [[mana]] directly, the caster defines a **set of instructions embedded in geometry**, and [[mana]] executes those instructions upon activation.
This makes Weave Casting more consistent than Flux Casting, but less adaptable to changing conditions.
## Structure Types
Weave Casting uses two primary structural states:

### 1. Active Weave (Drawn Circles)
A circle or rune structure constructed during active casting.

- Built under time pressure
- Requires continuous or staged construction
- Vulnerable to interruption
- Becomes functional only when fully completed

Active Weaves represent “in-progress computation.”

### 2. Woven (Prepared Circles)
A fully completed structure prepared before activation.

- Fully constructed in advance
- Requires only [[mana]] input to activate
- Extremely stable and consistent
- Cannot be modified during activation
Woven represent “compiled spell systems.”

## Activation Principle
Weave Casting does not release [[mana]] immediately upon construction.
Instead:
> [[mana]] must be deliberately introduced into a completed structure to trigger execution.

Once activated, [[mana]] flows through the encoded structure and produces the defined effect.
The structure determines:

- effect type (fire, healing, force, etc.)
- magnitude scaling (based on mana input)
- spatial behaviour (range, radius, travel rate)
- conditional logic (if defined by runes)

## Rune Logic System
Runes act as **instructional nodes** within a weave.
Each rune encodes a specific parameter, such as:
- elemental behaviour (fire, frost, lightning)
- magnitude scaling (damage, intensity, force)
- spatial rules (radius, direction, travel rate)
- conditional modifiers (on hit, on contact, delayed trigger)
A completed weave is effectively a **network of instructions interpreted by [[mana]] flow**.

## Stability vs Flexibility
Weave Casting exists between Flux Casting and pure ritual magic:
- More stable than Flux Casting (structured constraints reduce variance)
- Less flexible than Flux Casting (predefined behaviour limits adaptation)
- More flexible than rigid ritual magic (can include variable inputs like mana scaling or conditional runes)

## Disruption and Failure
Weave Casting can fail in two primary ways:

### 1. Structural Disruption
If the weave is incomplete or damaged before activation:
- [[mana]] cannot interpret the structure correctly
- spell fails or partially resolves
- unstable discharge may occur depending on construction stage

### 2. Input Instability
If [[mana]] is introduced improperly or inconsistently:
- effect may scale incorrectly
- targeting or behaviour may distort
- partial activation may occur
Unlike Flux Casting, failure in Weave Casting is usually **localized and structured**, not fully chaotic.

## Casting Identity
Weave Casting is:
- Structured mana programming
- Geometry-based instruction encoding
- Stable execution system
- Medium-speed casting framework
- Foundation for combat sigils and pre-prepared magic
