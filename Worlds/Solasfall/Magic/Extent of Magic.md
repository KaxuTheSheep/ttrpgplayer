Mana manipulates what exists. It cannot create from nothing. Every magical effect — however dramatic — is the acceleration, suppression, or redirection of a process already present in the physical world. The scope of what magic can achieve is therefore not defined by categories or schools of thought, but by the caster's understanding of the process they are trying to influence and their capacity to direct mana precisely enough to achieve it.

This document outlines what is physically possible under that principle, from straightforward manipulation to the most demanding feats ever achieved.

## The Core Principle

Mana interacts with physical properties: temperature, pressure, kinetic energy, oxidation rate, cellular activity, light wavelength, electrical conductivity, and so on. Vivant excitation increases these properties in whatever mana contacts. Mordant excitation decreases them. The degree of change scales with the intensity and precision of the mana applied.

A fire spell does not conjure flame. It increases the oxidation rate and thermal energy of existing fuel until combustion occurs. A healing spell does not restore flesh by will — it accelerates cellular repair processes already present in a living body. Darkness is not summoned — light wavelengths are suppressed until none remain visible. Every magical effect follows this logic without exception.

The practical limit is not mana supply. The World's Breath ensures ambient mana is effectively inexhaustible under normal conditions. The limits are the caster's understanding of the process being manipulated, the precision of their control, and in extreme cases the soul's capacity to conduct the required flow without damage.

## What Is Possible

### Physical Property Manipulation
The most accessible and well-understood category. Increasing thermal energy produces heat and fire. Decreasing it produces cold. Increasing kinetic energy accelerates objects or creatures. Increasing pressure can crush or propel. Decreasing oxidation rate smothers flame. These effects are achievable by trained mages with moderate understanding of the underlying process and represent the foundation of most practical magic.

### Light Manipulation
Light is a physical phenomenon with manipulable properties — wavelength, amplitude, and polarisation. A mage who understands light can shift its colour, suppress it entirely to produce genuine darkness, amplify it, or redirect it. This is not illusion. The light itself is physically altered. A darkness effect is real absence of light, not perceived darkness. A colour shift changes the actual wavelength reaching the eye.

The sky layer of the Veil operates on this principle at city scale — a sustained Vivant mana field physically manipulating light across the entire interior to simulate weather, time of day, and atmospheric conditions.

### Biological Manipulation
Cellular activity is a physical process and therefore subject to mana manipulation. Lifebinders accelerate healing by increasing cellular repair rates. The same principle applied inversely can suppress biological function — slowing metabolism, inhibiting cellular activity, or accelerating decay. The complexity of the target organism determines the precision required. Manipulating a simple organism is straightforward. Manipulating a specific tissue in a complex creature without affecting surrounding systems requires considerable understanding of that organism's biology.

### Neurological Manipulation
Neurological activity is biological activity. In principle, mana can influence it. In practice, the brain is the most complex structure known, and meaningful manipulation requires an understanding of neurological processes that does not currently exist in any documented form. The theoretical ceiling is significant — altering thought, perception, emotion, or behaviour through direct mana manipulation of brain activity. The practical ceiling is effectively zero. No mage has achieved reliable neurological manipulation. It remains a known implication of the system rather than a realised discipline.

### Spatial Manipulation
Space itself has physical properties that mana can influence. At small scales this is achievable but difficult to control — a trained practitioner can force open a brief, unstable tear in local space, connecting two nearby points directly. The Velkhari demonstrate this capability through their connection to Rift energy, creating short-range spatial rifts as a natural expression of their physiology rather than trained casting. For a conventional mage the same effect is theoretically possible but far harder to stabilise.

At larger scales — cross-dimensional or world-level connections — spatial manipulation alone is insufficient. The mana required to force a stable cross-dimensional rift exceeds what any individual soul can conduct. More importantly, dimensional boundaries have natural weak points where the barrier between connected worlds is thin. Large-scale rifts are only reliably achievable at these weak points, where the mana requirement drops to something manageable. The Hollowdeep connection exploited by the Riftconjurors every four weeks depends entirely on such a weak point existing between Solasfall's world and the Hollowdeep's web.

## Hard Limits

**Creation from nothing** is impossible. Mana manipulates existing matter and energy. A fire spell without fuel produces nothing. A healing spell on a corpse with no viable cells achieves nothing. Magic amplifies and redirects — it does not generate.

**Radiant and Umbral effects** cannot be produced by trained mages regardless of skill, understanding, or mana supply. These forces originate exclusively from deities and can only be channelled through Followers whose soul-current is synchronised with a deity's mana construct. No amount of conventional casting accesses them.

## The Upper Limits — Rifts and the Veil

The most demanding magical achievements on record both involve the manipulation of fundamental physical forces at scales no individual caster could sustain alone.

### Rifts
Forcing open a stable cross-dimensional rift at a weak point requires coordinated mana input far beyond a single soul's conducting capacity. The Riftconjurors achieve this through combined effort and institutional infrastructure, channelling mana from multiple sources into a single sustained effect. Even so, the process requires temporarily weakening the Veil — the two operations compete for the same mana environment, and the destabilisation is a known and accepted cost.

A rift is not a portal conjured from nothing. It is a forced, stabilised exploitation of a spatial weak point — the boundary between two connected worlds made temporarily passable by overwhelming the natural resistance at that point with coordinated Vivant mana. Closing it requires either withdrawing that mana input or actively reinforcing the boundary with Mordant mana to suppress the opening.

### The Veil
The Veil represents the most complex sustained magical structure in existence and the only working example of deliberate time manipulation. Its primary function — suppressing the time equilibrium pull from Solasfall's rift contact with a hyper-accelerated world — is achieved through a vast interlayered network of magic circles sustaining a Mordant mana field across the entire city. Time is a physical property. Decreasing it requires Mordant excitation of sufficient intensity to counteract the equilibrium pressure pulling Solasfall's time rate toward synchronisation with the faster connected world.

The scale of mana flow required to seal the Veil's structure during its original creation was beyond what any soul should conduct. The King's Mana Overload during that ritual was not an accident or a miscalculation — it was the predictable result of conducting a volume of mana no individual nervous system could handle without permanent damage. The Veil held. His conducting ability did not.

Active time manipulation beyond passive suppression — accelerating or reversing time deliberately — would require excitation intensities far exceeding anything the current infrastructure produces. The theoretical possibility exists. The practical means do not.
