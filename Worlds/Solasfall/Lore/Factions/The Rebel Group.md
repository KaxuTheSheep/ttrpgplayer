### Overview
The Rebel Group is [[Solasfall]]'s loosest and least understood faction — not a single organisation but a name applied, by outsiders and insiders alike, to a scattered network of grassroots cells united by opposition to the Ministry of Balance and, more broadly, to a system that taxes the poor to fund a miracle they're told they should be grateful for. Nobody outside the movement — and few within it — can say with confidence whether it has one leader, several, or none at all. That uncertainty isn't an accident.

### Composition & Recruitment
Overwhelmingly grassroots. Recruitment draws almost entirely from the Slum and Commoner Districts: families broken by Weighing executions, debtors who fled the Gilded Chain, workers who watched a neighbour conscripted into a [[Hollowdeep]] expedition to cover a quota they couldn't pay. Grief and debt are the movement's real recruiters, not ideology.

### Structure
Cells operate in near-total isolation from one another. A member of one cell may not know whether other cells exist, let alone who runs them. This produces the movement's defining mystery: is there a hidden coordinating hand, or is "the Rebel Group" simply dozens of unconnected groups sharing a name and an enemy? Both rebels and the authorities who hunt them are genuinely divided on the answer.

### Internal Politics — The [[Veil]] Fracture
The movement's one well-known internal divide, discussed openly even among sympathetic citizens: some rebels believe true freedom lies beyond the [[Veil]], not understanding or refusing to believe the time acceleration waiting outside. Others know exactly what the [[Veil]] holds back and fear that weakening it for any reason — even to strike at the Ministry — courts total catastrophe. This split occasionally turns cells against each other as much as against the city, and no unifying rebel doctrine has ever resolved it.

### Tactics
Sabotage of Ministry tax infrastructure, disruption of Weighing collection routes, freeing condemned debtors before Execution Day, and targeted killing of Gilded Chain enforcers where retaliation can be afforded. Counterpropaganda — pamphlets and word-of-mouth attacking the "Balance is Survival" doctrine as extortion dressed in civic virtue — is as much a [[Weapon|weapon]] as any blade.

### Relationships
- **The Ministry of Balance** — the primary target and the thing that gives the movement whatever coherence it has.
- **Order of the Gilded Chain** — direct, frequent, and lethal enemies.
- **Order of the Veiled Watch** — the Rebels' most dangerous hunter; cell structure exists largely because of them.
- **The Thieves' Guild** — informal, situational cooperation on smuggling, forged papers, and safehouses, never a formal alliance.
- **Cultists** — no ideological alignment, occasional overlap in membership, treated with wariness by rebels who don't want the movement's grievances confused with heresy.

### Public Perception
Sympathy is real but quiet in the poorer districts — most citizens who'd never join a cell still won't inform on one. The wealthy and the Ministry itself frame the Rebels as chaos for its own sake, criminal opportunists exploiting the Weighing's hardship rather than responding to it. Nobody actually knows the true shape of the organisation, and that uncertainty is treated as its own kind of threat.

### Symbol & Motto
No single sanctioned symbol — cells mark territory or solidarity with whatever improvised sign suits them, a broken scale scratched into stone being the most recurring, a deliberate mockery of the Ministry's own emblem. No agreed motto exists publicly.
