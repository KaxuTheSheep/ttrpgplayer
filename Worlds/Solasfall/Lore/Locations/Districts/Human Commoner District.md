The pulse and labour of Solasfall beats strongest here. The Commoner District bridges the gap between nobility and destitution — where ambition wrestles with exhaustion, and the hum of daily life hides quiet dissent. Craftsmen, clerks, teachers, and traders fill its crowded streets, maintaining the city’s order even as they chafe beneath its weight.

Workshops spill steam into the alleys, guild banners hang over public courtyards, and communal shrines gather both prayer and gossip. It is a place of shared struggle — neither cursed nor blessed — where families dream of rising above their means, and whispers of rebellion pass through tired hands.

In its heart, small schools and trade halls promise opportunity, while tax collectors and preachers ensure loyalty. The district’s true form — whether a wide ring or narrow strip between power and poverty — remains uncertain, but its purpose is clear: to keep Solasfall running, even as it slowly breaks under the strain.
