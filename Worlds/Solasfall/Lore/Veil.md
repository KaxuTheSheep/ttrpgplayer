The Veil is an immense magical structure encasing [[Solasfall]], separating it from the accelerated decay of the world beyond its boundaries. To most citizens it appears as a natural sky — sun, clouds, stars, and weather cycling in familiar rhythms. In truth it is two distinct systems operating in concert: a barrier that keeps time inside [[Solasfall]] from racing toward ruin, and a controlled sky layer that the Royal Mages actively operate to simulate and manipulate conditions above the city.

## What It Does
The Veil's primary function is to suppress the runaway time acceleration bleeding in from the world outside, keeping conditions inside [[Solasfall]] close to normal. Without it, the city would gradually be dragged toward the same fate as the Fallows.

Separate from that barrier, the Royal Mages maintain a sky layer — a manipulation of light across the interior of the Veil that produces the appearance of sun, cloud, stars, and weather. This is not illusion; it is a genuine, continuously managed alteration of light itself. The day-night cycle is standard, weather is adjusted for agriculture and public events, and sunrise or sunset can be delayed or advanced. A noble family hosting an event under a specific sky can petition — and pay — for those conditions to be arranged.

## What Powers It
The Veil's foundation is a vast interlayered network of [[Magic|magic]] circles — a primary circle encompassing the entire city with hundreds of smaller interlocking circles nested within it, each drawing in ambient [[Mana|mana]] and channelling it into the overall structure.

Mordant Stones harvested from the [[Hollowdeep]] provide the baseline [[Mana|mana]] the network requires. Mages stationed at the circle's anchor points regulate how much [[Mana|mana]] moves through each section, correcting imbalances as they arise. The Veil's continuous operation demands enormous quantities of Mordant Stones, and the Ministry of Balance manages procurement and funding through taxation and resource tithes — the reason the Weighing exists at all.

## Staffing & Operations
The Veil's circle network operates with roughly twenty to thirty mages on shift at any given time, drawn from a larger rotation pool combining Royal Mages and senior institutional mages. Routine monitoring is handled by senior institutional mages; Royal Mages take primary responsibility during the four-week renewal ceremonies, emergency corrections, and sky layer operations.

## Instability & The [[Rift]] Cycle
Every four weeks, the Royal Mages deliberately weaken the Veil to force open the controlled [[Rift|rift]] to the [[Hollowdeep]]. During this window the barrier's field is partially disrupted. Citizens experience this as distortions in the sky — flickering light, brief crimson flashes, distorted shapes where the sky layer fails to compensate. Some report sounds with no source. The official designation is *[[Mana|mana]] storms*, attributed to natural fluctuation.

Full renewal of the Veil's structure occurs on the same four-week schedule. These ceremonies are citywide events drawing both reverence and unease — the moments when the Veil's fragility is most visible.

## Rumours & Misconceptions
Among the lower districts, persistent rumours claim the Veil is sustained not by [[Mana]] Stones and rotating mages but by souls — mages bound into its structure, their consciousness trapped inside the barrier. This is false. The Veil runs on infrastructure and shift operators, not sacrifice. The Royal Mages have never formally denied it, though — a population that believes the Veil demands that kind of cost is a population less likely to question its maintenance fees.

## Public Perception
To most citizens the Veil is a miracle — the great shield that allows civilisation to exist. Children are raised under its painted stars. Poets praise its ever-living dawn. The renewal ceremonies are occasions of genuine reverence.

The sky layer reinforces this. A population that watches the sun rise and set on a familiar rhythm, that sees rain come when crops need it, that lives under a sky that feels *managed* rather than arbitrary — that population does not question the Veil's necessity. This is not accidental.
