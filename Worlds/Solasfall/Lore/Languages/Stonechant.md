Stonechant is a low, resonant language with deep tones and slow rhythm, reflecting the Drenek’s sturdy nature. It is spoken in measured phrases, often accompanied by ritualistic chanting. Their written form consists of carved runes and glyphs etched into stone or wood, meant to last generations.
### Sound Profile
Stonechant is deep, resonant, and heavily weighted in its phonetic structure. Speech is slow and deliberate, with extended vowel durations and strong emphasis on low-frequency consonants, producing a grounded, echoing quality even in close conversation.

Consonant clusters are common but articulated with separation rather than [[Speed & Resolution|speed]], giving the language a “carved” auditory texture. Intonation is relatively flat compared to other languages, with meaning carried primarily through rhythm, repetition, and controlled pauses rather than pitch variation.

In ritual or formal contexts, speech often transitions into chant-like delivery, where repetition and cadence reinforce meaning and social or spiritual intent. The overall impression is one of permanence and physical presence, as if the language itself is designed to endure like the materials it is written into.
