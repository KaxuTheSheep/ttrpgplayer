Cawscript is the chattering and mimicry-based language of the Kenku, combining sounds, gestures, and written glyphs that resemble bird tracks. It is versatile and adaptive, often incorporating borrowed words and phrases from other languages. Written Cawscript is common within Kenku districts and among scholars interested in their culture.
### Sound Profile
Cawscript is fast, erratic, and highly expressive. It consists of rapid chirps, caws, whistles, clicks, trills, and perfectly imitated sounds woven together with body language. Pitch, rhythm, and timing carry as much meaning as the sounds themselves, allowing the same vocalisation to convey different ideas depending on its delivery.

Because Kenku excel at mimicry, Cawscript readily absorbs words, accents, and noises from other languages, often reproducing them with uncanny accuracy before blending them into native speech. Conversations can include the creak of a door, the clang of metal, a person's voice, or the call of another bird if those sounds communicate the intended meaning. To non-speakers, it can sound chaotic and fragmented, while fluent listeners perceive it as precise and richly nuanced.
