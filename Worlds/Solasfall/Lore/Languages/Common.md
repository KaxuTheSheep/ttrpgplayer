The lingua franca of Solasfall, Common is spoken by virtually all inhabitants regardless of race. It is the language of trade, governance, and daily life across the city’s diverse districts. Written Common is taught widely in schools and used in official documents, making it the default language for outsiders and newcomers.
### Sound Profile
Common is phonetically neutral and deliberately accessible, designed for ease of acquisition across diverse linguistic backgrounds. It tends toward moderate syllable length, balanced consonant-vowel distribution, and avoids extreme consonant clusters or tonal dependency.

Speech is generally smooth and moderately paced, with clear enunciation prioritised over ornamentation. Intonation carries meaning in informal contexts, but most semantic load is carried lexically rather than through pitch or breath effects.

The language tolerates borrowing heavily, so its phonetic character shifts slightly depending on district or speaker background, but it always remains within a stable “neutral core” that prevents it from drifting into any single cultural phonology.
