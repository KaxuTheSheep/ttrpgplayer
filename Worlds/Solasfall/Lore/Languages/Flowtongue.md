Flowtongue mimics the fluidity of water, with a smooth, rolling phonetic structure. It is spoken softly, often with long pauses and breaths reflecting Murks’ amphibious nature. The written form uses flowing, wave-like characters that can be difficult to replicate outside Murk communities.
### Sound Profile
Flowtongue is smooth, continuous, and low-impact in articulation. Speech flows in elongated syllabic chains with minimal hard stops, creating a sense of uninterrupted motion. Consonants are softened or reduced where possible, and vowel sequences are frequent and often blended through glides.

Timing is as important as phoneme selection: pauses are deliberate and meaningful, often functioning as structural boundaries rather than hesitations. The overall effect is a language that feels “carried” rather than spoken, with a gentle rise-and-fall rhythm that mirrors breath and movement through water.
