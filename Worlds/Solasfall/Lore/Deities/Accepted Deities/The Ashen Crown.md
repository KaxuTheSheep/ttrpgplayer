**Titles:**  
Lady of Ruin, The Warmaiden, Glory’s End

**Domains:**  
War, Glory, Ruin

**Symbols & Iconography:**  
The Ashen Crown is depicted wearing a jagged crown of blackened steel, often with flames or scorched motifs trailing from her figure. Her symbol is a crown or helmet engulfed in ash and embers, sometimes etched into Shields or Weapons.

**Mythos:**  
She embodies the grandeur and tragedy of war, teaching that true glory comes through sacrifice, courage, and the acceptance of loss. Legends recount her leading doomed battles where soldiers’ bravery turned the tide of fate, only for the battlefield to claim them. Her tales emphasize honour in conflict, the inevitability of ruin, and the transcendence that can follow noble Death.

**Influence & Presence:**  
Temples and shrines to the Ashen Crown are often located near barracks, training grounds, or battlefields. Her presence inspires martial prowess and valor, rewarding acts of courage and punishing cowardice. Worship is intertwined with martial discipline, strategic insight, and ritualised duels or commemorations.

**Worshippers:**  
Followed by soldiers, rebels, and those who seek noble Death. Her adherents often dedicate themselves to mastering combat and embracing the inevitability of loss. See Ashen Crown Sanctum.
