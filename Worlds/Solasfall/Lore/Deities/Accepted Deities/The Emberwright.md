**Titles:**  
The Forgefather, Keeper of Sparks, Architect of Flame

**Domains:**  
Craft, Fire, Invention

**Symbols & Iconography:**  
Depictions of the Emberwright vary from a cloaked smith with molten veins to a radiant, faceless figure framed in a halo of flame. His symbol is a hammer striking an anvil, from which a spark forms the shape of a star. Shrines burn with ever-burning coals — it is said that to let the flame die is to invite his silence.

**Mythos:**  
The Emberwright forged the first tools from the bones of fallen stars, gifting mortals the power to shape the world as he once shaped the heavens. Yet his lessons were double-edged — for every forge that builds, another burns. His followers say invention is divine, but hubris is sin, and that the Emberwright watches all who create with both pride and caution.

**Influence & Presence:**  
His temples double as forges and workshops, echoing with the rhythmic song of hammers. The Emberwright’s influence reaches every artisan, craftsman, and innovator who seeks to leave a mark upon the world. He blesses creation, but demands purpose — careless invention is seen as an affront to his divine craft.

**Worshippers:**  
Honoured by blacksmiths, engineers, artificers, and inventors, the Emberwright’s faithful believe creation itself is worship. Their work is their prayer, their tools their scripture. See Emberwright Sanctum.
