_In Solasfall, Faith is power — literally. The Deities exist only through Faith, and their influence on the world grows or fades in proportion to their followers. This has shaped a unique spiritual ecosystem: one of open choice, quiet competition, and pragmatic worship._

### Worship in the City
- The state is secular. There is no official religion endorsed or enforced by the King or Royal Advisors.
- Citizens are free to follow any Deity aside from Forbidden Deities, including multiple at once — though doing so comes with spiritual limitations.
- Many commoners casually revere multiple Deities, praying to  The Candle Mother for warmth, then The Blind Shepherd for safe passage, and The Verdant Hand for a good harvest.
- This practical, multi-faith belief is common among non-followers who seek blessings from various Deities without fully committing exclusive faith to any single deity.

### Power of Faith
- Deities draw power from pure, undivided belief.
- A believer who devotes themselves entirely to one deity offers greater divine sustenance than someone who splits their worship.
- Deities do not grant Divinity to multi-faith believers.
- This distinction explains why:
	- Casual believers hedge their bets with multiple Deities to receive weaker Blessings from several sources they have faith in.
	- True followers and priests must offer exclusive faith to receive and maintain divine gifts.
	- This leads to a quiet but intense pressure:
- Sanctums incentivize exclusive devotion.
- Priests actively campaign for loyal converts, not just casual worshippers.
- Monotheistic Followers often rise higher in Sanctum's hierarchies and are more likely to receive Divinity.

### Courts of Deities 
In Solasfall, worship is diverse and pragmatic. The city recognizes a set of **Accepted Deities** and a handful of **Forbidden Deities**, each with unique domains and followers.
#### Accepted Deities
1. The Blind Shepherd — God of Death, Law, Guidance, Vows, and Exchange
2. The Candle Mother — Goddess of Home, Light, and Love
3. The Verdant Hand — God of Harvest, Growth, and Luck
4. The Emberwright — God of Craft, Fire, and Invention
5. The Pale Bloom — Goddess of Suffering, Endurance, and Tragic Beauty
6. The Ashen Crown — Goddess of War, Glory, and Ruin
#### Forbidden Deities
Forbidden Deities exist outside the sanctioned Sanctum system. Their worship is clandestine, illegal, or socially condemned. Belief in them often carries risk, as public knowledge of devotion can lead to persecution, imprisonment, or death. Despite this, some still seek their power in secret, drawn to promises of forbidden knowledge, obsession, or unholy gifts.
1. The Maw Below — God of Hunger, Obsession, and Insatiable Desire
2. The Red Thorn — Goddess of Forbidden Love, Secrets, and Memory Manipulation
3. The Hollow Shade — God of Shadows, Lost Souls, and Forgotten Truths
