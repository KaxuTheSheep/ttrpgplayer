# Reading Order — Where to Start

This is a suggested order, not a mandatory one. Core mechanics first, then Solasfall setting material. Within Solasfall, "Essential" is what you need before character creation and session one; "Reference" is what you'll want to dip into as it becomes relevant in play rather than read cover to cover.

## 1. Core Rules (system-agnostic)

Read roughly in this order:

1. [[The Core Dice]] — 2d10, DNs, Advantage/Disadvantage, Crits
2. [[Attributes]] — the six stats
3. [[Skills]] — training levels, skill list
4. [[Character Creation]] *(Solasfall-specific but needed early)*
5. [[Actions]] — Full/Half Actions, Reactions
6. [[Rounds]] — Declaration/Resolution phases
7. [[Speed & Resolution]] — turn order
8. [[Sturdiness]], [[Blood]], [[Sanity]] — the three pools
9. [[Death]], [[Injuries]] — what happens when pools hit zero
10. [[Conditions]], [[Afflictions]] — status effects
11. [[Attacks]], [[Ranged Attacks]] — how hits resolve
12. [[Defensive Stances]] → [[Block]], [[Parry]], [[Dodge]]
13. [[Range & Threat]], [[Close-Quarters]], [[Facing]]
14. [[Movement Types]], [[Vision]]
15. [[Rest & Recovery]]

### Equipment (reference when buying gear)
- [[Weapon]] / [[Weapons]] / [[Weapon Traits]]
- [[Armour]], [[Shields]]
- [[Carrying Limits]], [[Ammunition]], [[Toxins]]

### Combat extras (reference as they come up)
- [[Grapple]], [[Unarmed]], [[Off-Hand & Dual-Wielding]]
- [[Brace]], [[Heavy Attack]], [[Ready Action]], [[Drawing & Stowing]]
- [[Action Fatigue]], [[Reactions]]

## 2. Solasfall — Essential (before session one)

1. [[Solasfall]] — the city, in broad strokes
2. [[Races]] — pick your race, then read your race's own page in full (traits + racial web)
3. [[The Governance of Solasfall]] — King, Royal Advisors, who's actually in charge
4. [[The Ministry of Balance]] and [[The Weighing]] — why everyone's afraid of tax day
5. [[Factions]] (overview list) — just enough to know what exists
6. [[Faith]] and [[Religion]] — you'll need this for Faith Point allocation at creation
7. [[Magic]] (overview) — only if you're playing a mage; skip otherwise for now
